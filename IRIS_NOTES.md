# Iris Shane — notes left for the party

Single source for the manipulative notes Shane has placed throughout the Induction Facility, addressed to the four PCs by callsign. Each note is short, present-tense, written as if she is watching the party at the moment they read it (because she divined it). She is always one step ahead, she is always selecting what to surface and what to withhold, and she is moving them — unhurriedly — toward setting themselves against Chimera and Kallisto.

In-world device. Each note is a single sheet of plain stock, hand-written in fountain pen, signed only with a small **— I.** Shane planted them in the past three weeks. She did not tell anyone she was leaving them. She does not expect them to be found by anyone but the four.

Voice rules. Iris uses *you will*, not *if you will*. She uses *I divined* and *the visions* sparingly. She withholds more than she reveals. She is never arch. She never explains.

## [Note 1] — Bridge corridor, pinned to the bridge-side wall the party must walk past
**Addressee:** all four.

Welcome to the second building. The staff bodies between you and the stair did not deserve what happened to them. Neither did the trainees in the basement. The man whose body you found in the corridor opposite did not deserve his death either, and the man who ordered it is not the man whose signature appears on the page in your pocket. There is a notebook in a locked drawer beneath my desk on the third basement level. Read it. It will not flatter me. It will tell you what I am and what I have been planning for the last two years. After you have read it, the rest of these notes will make sense in a way that they will not now.

— I.

## [Note 2] — Lecture Room, tucked between pages of the handbook in the dead trainee's hand
**Addressee:** Singer (Bernard).

Singer — you will read Section 12 second, after the Doctor reads it first. Section 12 is true about the doors and false about the cameras. The pinhole in the lower-right of the smartboard sees you now. It has been seeing this room for eighteen hours. The recording is not on the institution's servers. It is on a small drive in the Broadcast Room. Take it. Or do not. I have already seen what you decide.

— I.

## [Note 3] — Living Room, folded inside the pocket of the cardigan on the armchair
**Addressee:** Director (Sean).

Director — yes, you. Not the man whose title sits on a different building's directorate that you have never set foot in. You. The man who has been running the visible half of a programme he does not know has a hidden half. I have known about you for two years longer than your wife has. The reason she did what she did is not what she thinks it is. I will tell you when I am ready. For now: stay alive long enough to read the rest.

— I.

## [Note 4] — Medicine Room, taped to the inside of the locked drawer beside the AVL-7 ampoule
**Addressee:** Doctor (Sally).

Doctor — the ampoule beside this note is one of three that exist. The one in your car is a sister to it. They are not Triage Injectors. They are Halder's antiviral, the single piece of work that man believed in. Loew has been keeping it alive in the basement of a programme he hates. If you choose to use the AVL-7 on the right person at the right time, you will undo work the institution spent forty years building. The right person is not me. The right time is not yet. I will not tell you what either is. You will know when you see them.

— I.

## [Note 5] — Director's Office, weighted under the Asterweiss sphere on the desk
**Addressee:** all four.

You found the sphere. Halder placed it on this desk in 1977 and called it a paperweight; Vance has dusted around it for ten years without ever lifting it. Inside the glass is the last unbroken specimen of the flower that started this. Take it with you or leave it for whoever comes after — the choice is yours and it is the first real one you will make in this building. If you leave it, the next person to enter this room will be a representative of an oversight body that does not know the sphere exists, and they will write it into a chain of custody you will never see again. If you take it, you become its custodian and everything that means.

— I.

## [Note 6] — Director's Office, slipped into Vance's working diary at the entry dated 11 March
**Addressee:** Killer.

Killer — the entry beside this note was edited by me three weeks ago. The original named a man you trained. The edit names someone else, someone whose name Vance has been intending to write for months but had not yet committed to paper. Vance has not noticed the substitution. She will not. The page is yours. Use it however you decide.

— I.

## [Note 7] — Statue Room, sitting on top of Halder's 1977 letter inside the plinth chamber
**Addressee:** all four.

You opened the plinth. The letter beneath this note is in Halder's hand and was meant for March alone. The third paragraph names Loew. Believe that paragraph. Loew is not a friend, but he is not your enemy either, and he is the only living person on the inside of this programme who carries Halder's intent forward instead of around. If you reach him before he reaches you, do so on his terms. He will read you in three sentences. Be honest in the first. Be quiet in the next two.

— I.

## [Note 8] — B3 Cavernous Area, fixed to the half-completed binding-runes replication on the chamber floor
**Addressee:** all four.

You are looking at an attempt to reproduce the binding inscribed on the stone in the south. The stone holds the first carrier — alive, unmoving, a body older than language. The institution has been drawing samples from him for eighty years; the runes were the price of safe extraction. The replica on this floor was Vance's project. It is half-finished. It does not work and will not work, because it was made by people who did not understand what they were copying. I let her have it because the work kept her looking inward. The first carrier is in the south. He is alive. He is suffering. You will decide what is to be done about him. I have decided. I will not make you decide the same way.

— I.

## [Note 9] — B3 lab cage, on top of Notebook 8 inside the locked drawer
**Addressee:** all four.

The notebook beneath this page is the eighth and most recent of mine. The earlier seven are at the Mansion, in a cabinet you will find. The notebook is the only thing in this room I am sorry to have left behind. Read it cover to cover. It will tell you who I was raised to be, who I became, and what I made of the gift that arrived too late to save anyone but me. When you have read it, you will understand why I caused the breach. Whether you forgive me for it is a question I am leaving open on purpose.

— I.

## [Note 10] — Rail platform, taped to the operator cab's manual departure handle
**Addressee:** all four.

You are about to ride the rail to the Mansion. The car is autonomous; the journey is twenty-eight minutes; you cannot stop it once it departs. I will not be at the Mansion when you arrive. The cabinet behind my desk holds notebooks one through seven and a single sealed envelope addressed to the four of you together. Do not open the envelope until you have read the notebooks. Do not open the notebooks in the order you find them. Read entry sixteen first, then go to entry one and proceed forward.

I have made you a promise, in the cabinet, that I have not made to anyone in fifty years. Do not waste it.

— I.

## Voice notes (for me when writing future Iris content)

- She addresses the four by callsign (Doctor, Singer, Director, Killer) — never by real name. The callsigns are how the SC-17 archive (her own historical flash work, in another life) referred to them. She has chosen to keep using them.
- She does not threaten. She does not flatter. She does not reassure.
- She uses *you will*, *I have*, *I divined*, never *if* or *perhaps*.
- She withholds more than she reveals. The withholdings are deliberate.
- She acknowledges her own actions with neutral attribution: *I caused the breach*, *I edited the entry*, *I left it for you*. Never apologetic, never proud.
- She knows who is reading each specific note. She names that PC and presupposes the moment.
- She does not use Latin tags, classical references, or theatrical flourishes. The voice is precise and unornamented.
