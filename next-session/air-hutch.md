# Fork — THE HUTCH FLIGHT (out by air)

They call Wider's getaway pilot off his sat-phone and try to fly out *under* the net. Maximum spectacle, maximum risk — and it only happens if they talk a wary old man into it.

---

## Calling Hutch

On **Wider's sat-phone**, the saved contact **HUTCH — (603) 555-0172.** He answers expecting Wider:
> *(gravelly, wary)* "Joe? …That ain't Joe breathing. Who the hell is this, and why've you got Joe Wider's phone?"

**Who he is:** **Denny "Hutch" Hutchins**, late 60s, ex-Army aviator (Vietnam tail-end plus decades of contract flying), runs occasional off-book short-hops out of a hayfield strip in far-northern New Hampshire. Owes Wider old debts. Doesn't scare easy — but he's not suicidal.

**The negotiation — he needs three things, and the PCs have to give them:**
1. **Where's Joe?** They're calling on a dead man's phone. They have to account for Wider — and Hutch will *hear* a lie.
2. **Why would he fly into a federal no-fly zone** "that's been crawling with Black Hawks since yesterday"?
3. **What's in it for him** — cash, a debt called, a reason.

If they're straight and desperate (and can pay or trade), he'll do it — cursing the whole way, calling them every kind of fool. If they lie badly or threaten him, he hangs up and that door's closed.

## His plane (concrete)

A **Cessna 182 Skylane**, tail **N4427T**, robin's-egg blue, a patched seat and a dent in the cowl. Based at **the Hutchins strip** — a mowed line off a logging road near the Quebec border, no tower, no lights. **Four seats**; with four PCs plus Hutch, someone rides folded into the baggage shelf.

## The flight under the net

**The play:** Hutch flies **nap-of-the-earth** — treetop height, lights out, down the valleys — to stay under the radar net (the same trick Serrat's heli used to sneak *in*). It works *if* they stay low and lucky.

**The spectacle** (no tactics needed to feel it): grey dawn valleys, the little plane bucking in the downdrafts, Hutch swearing at the yoke — and overhead, **the net.** A **Black Hawk** working a search grid off to the east; once, high up, a **drone** like a slow black speck. The sky that hunted them an hour ago, and they're flying *through* it in a tin can.

**The risk (where the dice live):** climb too high, fly too long, or roll a bad break and the net catches the contact. Then —
> *(on Hutch's radio, a calm federal voice)* "Unidentified aircraft vicinity [area], you are in restricted airspace under federal TFR. Squawk and land immediately or you will be intercepted."
A Black Hawk swings onto an intercept vector. **Detection → forced down** (loops to `overland.md` or `captured.md`) or a white-knuckle run for the line.

## Per-PC hooks

- **Sean** — loves aircraft; co-pilots, or handles anything that closes on them (the sandbox).
- **Killer** — chaos: if a bird closes, he does something insane to it.
- **Sally** — keep Hutch and the plane alive if it takes fire or a bird-strike.
- **Bernard** — the calm in the cabin (his mist-thinning does nothing to weather, so no fog-bank trick): a **Bardic Inspiration** to Hutch on a white-knuckle maneuver, or simply holding the others together while the plane bucks.

## Player-question answers

- *"What's the plane — registration, look?"* → Cessna 182, **N4427T**, robin's-egg blue, dented cowl, patched seat.
- *"What does the radio say when we're spotted?"* → the intercept call above ("unidentified aircraft… restricted airspace under federal TFR… land immediately or be intercepted").
- *"Who is Hutch, really?"* → ex-Army aviator, Wider's old debt-holder, far-northern NH, flies off-book and asks few questions — but not *no* questions.
- *"Where does he take us?"* → wherever's *out* — across the line into Quebec, or to a quiet strip beyond the TFR; then he drops them and is gone, wanting never to see them again.

## Where it leads

Make it under the net → loose, across the line, pointed at Todd; Hutch vanishes. Get caught → forced down → `overland.md` / `captured.md`.
