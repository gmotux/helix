# The dream — *Inventory*

*The recovered memory of the **harvest** — the eventful 45 minutes buried in the blackout, between "lights out in the lecture hall" and "awake the next morning, changed, Kessler gone." It's not a vision; it *happened.* Run it as a long-rest nightmare (before the Todd session works well). Two jobs: **teach the table each other's kits** by forcing them to fight 1v1, and **plant the face to hate** — Kessler — as a real, findable antagonist. Grounded in canon: this is the **harvest** (`STORY.md` rupture day). No stakes; nothing carries out but the memory and the debt.*

---

## The set (so staging is fixed)
A long concrete hall, ceiling lost in dark, work-lights on stands. Down one wall: a row of **five reinforced activation tanks** — four with a PC suspended in each, facing out; the **fifth**, at the end, holds something larger that doesn't hang still. On the floor: **Kessler.** Between her and the tanks, facing her: **Iris.** Down the opposite wall: eight **guards**, a clean line on Iris (and the tanks behind her). The glass is **armoured** — it won't crack to stray fire. Hold that; it's load-bearing.

---

## Run it in beats

**1 — Out of the memory they own.** *(open in the lecture hall — the last frame before the dark)*
> You are suspended — warm, weightless, fluid against open eyes. Through curved glass an inch from your face: a hall too long to be a room, work-lights, armed figures still against the far wall, and **tanks** — five of them, a sleeping shape in each. It takes too long to understand you're looking *out* — that the shape in the next tank is **[the PC beside them]**, that you're in one too, and that you do not want to look straight at the fifth.

**2 — The two of them.**
> Two figures on the floor. One you know on sight — **Kessler**, white coat, hands folded, patient. The other you also know, though it stops your heart to see her: not the human face she wore as Greta, but the form she showed you the night she died — an **elf**, fine-boned and ageless, ears swept to points, eyes too old for the face that carries them. *Iris.* Standing. Breathing. Alive the way the dead are alive in a dream.

**3 — The argument.** *(warped, through fluid and glass)*
> **Iris:** "This conversation is meaningless. You won't stop it. I've walked every path out of this room, and in none of them do you stop. So I'll do it for you."
> **Kessler:** "Walk out, and your brother starts paying for it again. You remember the sounds he made."
> **Iris:** "He's past your reach Mara. I saw to that. That's the one future you don't get to touch."
> **Kessler:** *(a beat — then, to the guards)* "Then I'll take the one you forgot to guard. Your own."

**4 — The volley, and what Iris does with it.**
> The rifles come up and open up — sustained fire, across the hall, at her. Her hand's already up; a **shield** flares and the rounds skip off it, screaming away at angles. She isn't untouchable — two get through, one across the shoulder, one through the side, and she takes them on her feet. And she isn't only surviving the fire; she's *steering* it. Every round she turns, she turns into the **same four points** — one weak seam on each of your tanks — and the deflected rounds worry those seams, hit after hit after hit, the way you'd work a crack until it runs. Four tanks, four seams, and on some round you've lost count of, the armoured glass gives. Fluid bursts across the concrete and the four of you spill out — coughing, blind, helpless at the foot of the tanks.
> *(The fifth tank she never touches. **DM:** armoured glass doesn't break to stray fire — it broke because she walked deflected rounds into the same point until it failed, and put none of them through a body. No guard does that by luck. To Kessler it reads as flailing. The **Rogue**, who carries her memory, simply knows.)*

**5 — Inventory.**
> The guns don't stop. Iris keeps the shield up and angled — between the muzzles and the four of you bleeding-wet on the floor — because the instant she drops it to do anything else, the next burst goes into four people too dazed to crawl. So she can't reach Kessler. She can only stand in it, bleeding, and hold.
> Kessler doesn't even glance at the broken tanks. She lifts a **syringe — fluid glowing like a struck match** — and puts it into her own throat. *"Seeing the future doesn't mean you can change the inevitable."* Something opens behind her eyes, and she says the word — **"Lu nu-zu"** — and it doesn't go to Iris. It goes to the four of you on the floor. Your hands stop being yours.
> **Kessler:** *"They may be your creations, but they are **my** property. And no one messes with my merchandise but me."* She lifts a hand, and the guns go still.

**6 — Trial run.**
> She looks the four of you over like a clipboard. *"I suppose a trial run's well overdue. Let's see what she made of you."* Two fingers rise as if she's advancing a slide — and your body turns on the one beside you.
> And Iris, still on her feet in the wreckage, shield finally down, blood at her side — looks at you, and **smiles.** Not kindly. The grim, private smile of someone who's already seen how this ends and knows that you haven't. Then she looks up, at the one road only she can see, and the air claps shut where she stood.

### Round one — the duels (1v1)
On Kessler's gesture, they're set on each other: **Cleric vs Druid · Warlock vs Bard**, in parallel — alternate them, cinematic, don't grind initiative. **Full kits, no saves, no coaching.** They play to win for pride; the table sees all four kits, and the horror is being *made* to do it to a friend. Kessler reads them like specimens — *"Adequate." "He didn't expect that — good."* Two of them win; it doesn't matter who.

### Round two — the real test (4 vs The Fifth)
She's seen them apart. Now she wants them *together,* against something that isn't them — and she wants it **fair**, so no one can claim the stock underperformed tired. The guards put a needle in each of them: **a serum that simulates a full long rest.** HP back, slots back, the duel wiped clean. *No excuses.* Then she cracks the **fifth tank.**

> A fist, a shoulder, then something that used to be a person tears itself out through armoured glass four rifles couldn't crack — out wrong, still *rewriting itself* as it comes, roaring, already reaching. Kessler steps back to watch.

The four — fresh, and not their own — are turned on **The Fifth** (statblock below). **This fight is built to be lost.** It's tuned to grind four level-7 PCs even at full strength, and it punishes everything they do alone. Let them try; let it work them over; let them learn — by *losing* — that they survive this only as a unit, and that **this is what's waiting for whoever doesn't get the flower.** It is the failure mode with claws, and it crawled out of the tank *beside theirs.*

**It never resolves.** As the last of them goes down — or as The Fifth tears past them entirely — the dream gives one last frame: **Kessler, unbothered, almost pleased, watching it break loose.** *(That is why it's loose in the world: they were losing to it when the harvest collapsed, and it got out.)*

**Wake:** the surface, all at once — gasping in the dark, hands clenched, the word still in your teeth.

## THE FIFTH — the harvest-Tyrant *(dream boss · built to be lost)*
*A flagged candidate whose expression collapsed with no control and no flower — mutation made flesh. Name the person behind it at the table; the PCs only ever see the monster. Different lineage from the Rogue's S-0 Tyrant (collapse, not absorption); it reads* wrong *in its own way, and the Rogue can feel the difference.*

> **THE FIFTH** — Huge aberration (collapsed carrier), neutral evil
> **AC** 18 (writhing carapace) · **HP** 300 (24d12+144) · **Speed** 40 ft, climb 40 ft
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |----|----|----|----|----|----|
> | 22 (+6) | 16 (+3) | 22 (+6) | 7 (−2) | 14 (+2) | 16 (+3) |
>
> **Saving Throws** Str +11, Con +11, Wis +7, Cha +8
> **Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, slashing from nonmagical attacks
> **Senses** blindsight 30 ft, darkvision 120 ft, passive Perception 16 · **Languages** understands, cannot speak
> **Challenge** 15 (13,000 XP) · **Proficiency** +5
>
> ***Magic Resistance.*** Advantage on saves vs spells and magical effects.
> ***Legendary Resistance (3/Day).*** On a failed save, it may choose to succeed instead.
> ***Adaptive Hide.*** The first time each turn it takes damage of a given type, it gains **resistance to that type until the start of its next turn.**
> ***The Gene Keeps Writing.*** At the start of its turn it gains one **mutation** (pick or d4): **1** caustic weep — 10-ft aura, 7 (2d6) acid to anyone who starts its turn there this round; **2** spiked carapace — a creature that hits it with a melee attack takes 7 (2d6) piercing this round; **3** knits shut — regain 20 HP; **4** plating — +2 AC this round.
>
> **Actions**
> ***Multiattack.*** Two Claws and one Bite.
> ***Claw.*** *Melee* +11, reach 10 ft. *Hit:* 15 (2d8+6) slashing.
> ***Bite.*** *Melee* +11, reach 5 ft. *Hit:* 17 (2d10+6) piercing, and a Large-or-smaller target is **grappled (escape DC 17)**. On its next turn it can drag the grappled target up to 20 ft; only it can free them.
> ***Sundering Roar (Recharge 5–6).*** 30-ft cone. DC 17 Con save: **42 (12d6) thunder**, half on a success; a creature that fails is also **stunned** to the end of its next turn.
>
> **Reactions**
> ***Recoil.*** When a creature it can see within 30 ft casts a spell, it moves up to half its speed toward that creature without provoking.
>
> **Legendary Actions (3/turn, after another creature's turn)**
> - **Lash (1).** Reach-15 tendril: +11, 10 (2d6+3) bludgeoning.
> - **Prowl (1).** Move up to its speed without provoking.
> - **Mutate (2).** Gain another **mutation** immediately.

**Why every line is here (synergy *forced,* not requested):**
- **Adaptive Hide** is the diversify-or-fail engine: any single damage type drops to half after its first hit each round, so spamming one source (Eldritch Blast, double Spirit Guardians) stops paying — they have to mix and stack. *(Force/radiant resistance removed; Adaptive Hide alone carries it.)*
- **Magic Resistance + 3 Legendary Resistances + aberration type** mean control buys a *window,* never the fight: *Hold Person* fails outright by type; Hypnotic Pattern / Fear mostly bounce.
- **Sundering Roar** punishes clumping for auras and buffs; **Bite-grab + Prowl + Recoil** hunt stragglers and the back line. Nowhere is safe; nothing solos it.
- **The Gene Keeps Writing** changes the problem every round — no static plan survives.

**Running the loss (it's the point):** tune to your table and let it win. It should drop them one at a time, ugly, while they *finally* start trying to combine — and **cut off the instant the last falls or it breaks past them.** No real death, nothing spent (dream; the serum reset is in-dream only). What carries out is the lesson and the dread: *we lost — rested, and even half-together — and that thing is awake somewhere now.*

**Tuning notes (post-playtest):** output is capped at **~77 damage/round** (Multiattack 47 + three Lash legendaries 30; or Roar 42 + Lashes) — enough to grind a faceplanting party down, not a math-guaranteed TPK. It also holds its **Sundering Roar and all three Legendary Resistances in reserve**: in the sim it beat the party to ~half HP without spending either. So if your table plays out of its skin, *that's* your dial-up — start leaning on the Roar and burning LegRes — no rewrite needed.

## What it plants (and the canon under it)
- **The face to hate, findable.** Kessler ran the harvest, took them, *lost* them, and **walked out alive** before the strike. She runs straight through threads they're already pulling (Patel → Todd → the program). Hate with a map.
- **"She made you."** The "she" is **Iris.** The harvest *activated* them — Iris is why they're carriers at all, Kessler is who tried to own them. That's the gut-knot under the whole dream.
- **They're implicated in their own first crime scene.** They started emitting the field the instant they activated — so some of the dead they woke among at the summit were partly *their* doing. The mutation field they've been puzzling over (Wider, the motel) began *here.*
- **The dead tongue → the Todd payoff.** The chant is **"Lu nu-zu. Inim-zu, inim-mu"** ("You are not your own. Your will, my will"). Seed the same line into Todd's cassette translation so that, days later, Todd reads it off the tape and the table goes cold — *they heard it in the cave… no, the tank.*
- **The Fifth, loose.** The harvest's failure mode — a carrier with no control and no flower — is now a Tyrant **awake in the world** (it tore loose mid-fight as the harvest collapsed). It's the one piece of the harvest **the Committee knows escaped**, so *its* hunt and the hunt for the four become one dragnet. And it's the **mirror**: four of them, one bloom — *that* is the future of whoever doesn't get the flower. *(Name the person behind it; the PCs only meet the monster.)*
- **The domination threat.** Something can override their will and turn them on each other — which should scare them specifically, because Sally's CD is the leash on the Rogue's Tyrant. The dream is the proof that *someone can take the leash.* (Ties to the Listeners as the eventual domination antagonist.)

## Running notes
- **No metagame guardrails needed** beyond "this is a dream — nothing dies, nothing's spent." Let them brawl; the horror is being *made* to, not the damage.
- It's the **recovered harvest** (the eventful ~45 minutes of an overnight blackout), so it should feel like *footage,* not symbolism — the last thing they remember is the lecture hall going dark; this is what the dark was hiding.
- Keep Iris on a leash: she gets the argument, the deflection, and the smile — **no monologue.** She's not the villain and she doesn't compete with Kessler for the room.
