# Edmund Halder — the contact

*How the PCs reach Edmund (only ever **through Todd**), and — the point of this doc — **how he actually reacts.** He's a real ally and a real hazard at the same time: the most valuable friend they could make and the most watched man tied to the whole affair. Run him so both are true.*

---

## Who he is (run him from this)

- **Edmund Halder, ~53.** Chair of the Halder Foundation Board (inherited from his father in 2018). Runs the **Halder Genomics Initiative (HGI)** — a small antiviral lab at Boston University, his grandfather Everett's *original* ethical vision, kept deliberately **outside Kallisto's reach.**
- **Helix-positive from birth — and does NOT know it.** The gene runs latent in the Halder direct line. He has no idea. *(See DM-only; this matters more than anything he'll say.)*
- **Was a child in the 1979 paediatric trial** (one of eight) — came out clean; his grandfather pulled him early. **He doesn't know this either.**
- **Eight years of stonewalled questions.** Since taking the Board seat he's pushed on antiviral funding allocations; Gideon stonewalled him every quarter. He knew something was rotten long before the rupture — he just couldn't reach it.
- **Now: the Committee's most dangerous public enemy.** Post-rupture he went public, released Foundation records, called for a Congressional inquiry "above any classification ceiling," **named Klaus Esterhazy on television,** and sits as **special advisor** to that inquiry.
- **Character:** measured, sharp, stubborn, decent. An institutionalist who genuinely believes **daylight wins** — he has staked his life on it. Not naive anymore: he knows he's watched, knows he's a target, and has been learning tradecraft on the fly. Todd's wife once said he *"looked at us like we might be the same kind of strange he was"* — there's an unspoken sense of being an outsider in his own skin he's never had a name for.

## Where he is the moment they reach him

A few days past the escape. Edmund is at **peak exposure and peak surveillance:** fresh off naming Esterhazy, doing cooperation theatre on camera, feeding the inquiry. Around him, unseen by him:
- The **Committee watches him constantly** (they pressured a journalist off his HGI funding back in 2023; on a strategic scale they *will* eventually vote on whether to remove him).
- The **Foundation's own legal counsel is quietly running interference for the Committee** without his authorisation. Three Board members are Committee-aligned and resisting his disclosures.
- He is **privately back-channeling to Henrik Loew** already.

So: anyone who touches Edmund is touching a node with eyes all over it. That's the hazard baked into him.

## What he wants (and the clash that makes him dangerous to trust)

Edmund wants the **truth surfaced, the Committee broken, and the record protected.** When he grasps what the PCs are — **the single most valuable witnesses on the planet** — his instinct is immediate and sincere: *bring them in.* Lawyers, the inquiry, protective framing, daylight. **He believes that's how he keeps them alive.**

That is exactly the thing the PCs **cannot** do — they're invisible, presumed dead, carrying a field that kills the people around them, and (if they went loud) one positive ID from a capture-or-kill BOLO. **Edmund's best, most heartfelt offer is shaped like a trap.** The friction of dealing with him isn't suspicion — he's honest — it's that his solution could get them caged or killed, and he doesn't understand *why* at first, because he doesn't know about the field or how hot they are.

---

## The contact — how it realistically happens

**It only works through Todd.** Edmund is buried in cranks and bait now that he's the public truth-teller; a cold call claiming "the dead summit people are alive" reads as a Committee provocation or a lunatic. The thing that cuts through is **Todd's voice** — a man Edmund has trusted across six years of correspondence.

- **Channel:** not Edmund's public lines (watched). Todd reaches him the way old friends with something to hide do — a number from their private correspondence, a call placed from a clean phone. Edmund, already assuming surveillance, will steer it somewhere he controls (a callback, a specific quiet line, eventually an in-person he sets the terms of).
- **What Todd brings that no one else can:** he's a **Kallisto contractor and primary source** Edmund never knew he had — their letters were *"never about Kallisto, always about cognitive science."* Learning Todd was tangled in it the whole time lands on Edmund hard, and *that* is the thing that makes him believe the rest.
- **The PCs' choice:** how much do they let Todd reveal? Edmund can be told only "shelter my friend," or the whole impossible truth. The more he's given, the more he can do — and the more dangerous he becomes to them.

## How he reacts — in beats

1. **Guarded, testing.** First instinct is *this is a trap.* He verifies it's really Todd — shared, unfakeable detail (the São Carlos signing dinner, the meal Todd's wife cooked, a thread from their letters). Cold and careful until he's sure.
2. **The floor drops.** When he accepts it's Todd, *and* that Todd is hunted, *and* that there are living summit witnesses — the man who's been fighting blind for the truth suddenly has a primary source and four impossible witnesses. He gets very quiet, then very focused.
3. **All in — his way.** *"Come in. I can protect you. I have counsel, I have the inquiry, I can put you somewhere safe and on the record by tomorrow."* He means every word; he has just bet his own life on daylight and he wants to extend the cover to them.
4. **The collision.** They can't. When they refuse the safety he's offering, he doesn't understand — he pushes, because in his model *coming in* is survival and *staying dark* is suicide. He doesn't yet know about the field, or that surfacing might be a death sentence. **Play his frustration as love, not villainy.** Whatever they tell him to bridge that gap is a real disclosure with real consequences.
5. **What he settles for.** He'll take what they'll give. At minimum: **Todd, sheltered.** At most: a careful, deniable line of support to four ghosts he's decided to believe in.

## What he'll actually do

- **Shelter Todd — yes, and he can.** Todd is helix-negative (no field — safe to be near). Edmund has the means: lawyers, money, a public profile that's its own armour, and the inquiry's machinery. He won't stash Todd anywhere obvious (not HGI, not his home — all watched). Expect a discreet location, counsel attached, and likely **Todd folded into the inquiry as a protected "Kallisto contractor witness"** — which also weaponises Todd's testimony against the Committee. *(Cost: it puts Todd on the board as a named witness — a thing the Committee now has reason to reach for. Saving him this way also paints a target.)*
- **For the PCs:** he offers the institutional door — surfacing, protection, the witness chair. **Double-edged.** If they ever take it, it's the cleanest possible surface (factions: surface *via Edmund* → the most valuable witnesses alive; surface as the cabin's "Tyrant threat" → prey). If they don't, he becomes a careful, intermittent ally — information, a safe relay, a name that opens institutional doors — at the price of contact with a watched man.
- **He opens Loew.** Edmund is already back-channeling Henrik Loew; through Edmund the PCs gain a thread to the other institutional ally.

## The catch — why he's a hazard, not a safe harbour

- **Surveillance.** He is the most-watched man attached to this. Every contact risks the Committee, the feds, and the compromised Foundation counsel catching the edge of it.
- **His daylight instinct.** Left to his own judgment he will try to bring them into the light — the one move that can destroy them. An ally whose reflex is the wrong reflex.
- **He's a target himself.** The Committee has soft-moved against him before and may eventually move hard. Standing near Edmund is standing near someone with a target painted on him too.

## DM-only (do NOT spend these now)

- **Edmund is helix-positive.** If the PCs ever meet him **in person,** he does **not** sicken the way Todd/Wider do — the field doesn't touch him. **That's the tell that he's one of them.** Hold it. It's a future reveal worth a whole beat, and it makes Edmund, eventually, one of the only people on earth the PCs can safely stand beside.
- **The 1979 trial.** Edmund was one of the eight children; his grandfather pulled him clean. He has no idea. The names of the eight are still in a Kallisto file under Vogt's old initials — a thing that can surface *at* him later.
- **He is NOT omniscient.** He knows the cover story plus whatever he's pried loose and disclosed. He does **not** know about S-0, the gene, the field, the covert compartment, or his own nature. Don't let him explain the campaign — he's a fighter in the dark who happens to have a very bright torch pointed at the Committee.

## Player-question answers

- *"Can we trust him?"* → **Yes — and that's the problem.** He's honest and he's brave. But he believes the way to save you is to bring you into the light, and he's watched around the clock. Trustworthy, *not* safe.
- *"Will he hide Todd?"* → Yes, and he can do it properly. Likely via counsel + the inquiry's protective framing — which also turns Todd into a named witness the Committee now wants.
- *"Does he know who we are / what we are?"* → Only what you tell him through Todd. He has no idea about the field, S-0, or the gene — including in himself.
- *"Why does he care?"* → Eight years of stonewalled questions, a foundation built on his grandfather's ethics, and a public oath to break the people who did this. You're the proof he's been unable to reach.

## Forward hooks

- **Todd, parked and protected** — off the PCs' hands (and away from the field) without abandoning him.
- **The institutional door** stays open: a clean surface *through Edmund* whenever the PCs decide to stop being ghosts (the highest-value, highest-risk move on the board).
- **Loew** becomes reachable through Edmund's back-channel.
- **The long fuse:** Edmund is helix-positive and a 1979-trial child who knows neither. The first in-person meeting plants the seed; the file under Vogt's initials and the field-that-doesn't-touch-him pay it off later.
