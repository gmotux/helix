## Todd — who he is, how he reads (run him from this)

Fifty-eight. Brazilian-American, dual passport. Professor of linguistics and ancient history — Near Eastern dead languages (Sumerian, Akkadian, and older) — at USP São Carlos. **Helix-naive but sharp.**

- **Voice:** precise, dry, a little formal. Picks words carefully. Doesn't ramble.
- **What he wants:** to do the thing the letters told him to do (help them), hand off the weight he's carried for thirty-five years, and not die.

## The threat — the Committee cleaners (who & why, grounded)

**Why Todd:** He held **thirteen years of Patel's CRT cassettes** (`factions.md:553`) and consulted Kallisto's neurocognitive workstream for twenty-five years (STORY 369–383). Post-strike, the Committee is burying Chimera's research trail and silencing everyone who touched it. Todd is a contractor *in Kallisto's own records,* sitting on copies of the data. **Yates knew him by name since 1998.** He is the most findable loose end there is.

**What they are:** a Committee deniable cleanup team — **not** the federal net, not Serrat's military. Quiet professionals with **fake federal credentials** (the cover: walk Todd out "for a Bureau interview about Kallisto," put him in the Suburban, and he's never seen again). They are willing to do it ugly if quiet fails.

**What they know:** that Todd is meeting *someone* (they may have caught the Germany call, or just shadowed him here). **They do NOT know it's four summit-survivors** — those are dead. So the PCs arrive **unexpected**, which hands them the initiative.

**What they want:** Todd alive long enough to learn what he's said and to whom, then gone. 

**The cover cuts both ways:** if the PCs make it loud, the **Committee can't publicize this either** (a fake-fed snatch in a US hotel is *their* crime, not something they'll put on the news). Both sides want it quiet. So going loud here doesn't trigger the radiological cover or a federal bulletin — it's a **normal US crime scene: witnesses, cameras, local cops, and four faces caught on a hotel DVR.** Different bill than the cabin.

He names him, vouches, hands over how to reach him. **The PCs don't know Edmund; Todd is the bridge.** How Edmund plays the contact → **`edmund.md`**.

## THE CONVERSATION — what Todd tells them (the core)

### Q1 — "What about Patel? What did you know about CRT, him, and Kallisto?"

**The grounding (what Kallisto actually used a linguist for).** Todd reads dead languages — Sumerian, Akkadian, the tongue *under* them. That's why Kallisto wanted him, and it's the thread that ties the program straight to the tablet Bernard is carrying.

> "I consulted for Kallisto for twenty-five years. It began in '98 — a grant, dressed up as *'harmonic perception.'* The man who set it up was a virologist named **Yates.** From 2010 it was **Dev Patel.** Three or four times a year, for thirteen years, Patel sent me **audio tapes** and paid me very well to do the one thing I'm good at: tell him **what language was being spoken on them.**"

**What's on the cassettes (the concrete answer — say it plainly):**

> "Recordings of people. Sedated, or in some kind of trance — and *talking.* Speaking, like reciting, indiscernible words. But rarerly, some of these words, were in a language that's been dead for thousands of years — older than Sumerian, the parent tongue underneath it. The same family of script your great-grandfather brought back from the Pamir, Bernard. People with no schooling in dead languages, speaking a liturgy no one has spoken since before the wheel. I transcribed it. I translated what I could. Over thirteen years I built a partial lexicon out of those tapes — the only working key to that language that exists outside the tablets themselves."

His suspicion:

> "I told myself it was a study of glossolalia. It wasn't. You don't *recite* a tongue ten thousand years dead in your sleep. The words were coming from somewhere real. I knew it, and I cashed the cheques anyway."

**The Bernard gut-punch (to Bernard directly):**

> "In 2023 Patel asked me to refer my most unusual student into the programme. I sent him **you.** I didn't know what it was — God help me, Bernard, I sent you into the very thing I'd been translating."

**The anomalous voice — Bernard (the heart of it):**

> "And for three years there was one voice unlike all the others. The rest gave me fragments — a word, a broken line. This one was **fluent.** Whole passages, clean, unhesitating, like a priest who'd spoken it every day of his life. I only ever had a code, never a name. I think now the voice on those last tapes was **yours.**"

**The archive (forward hook):**

> "All of it — thirteen years of tape and every page of translation — is in a locked cabinet in my office in São Carlos. That's what these people want burned."

Patel's fate: Todd's been watching the news and assumes Patel died in the strike. **The PCs can confirm it** — they met Patel pre-rupture; he's presumed dead. (A small thing only *they* can give *him.*)

### Q2 — "What about this tablet?" (Bernard's fragment)

**No recognition puzzle — Todd already knows Bernard.** He's been Bernard's PhD supervisor for years. This isn't "prove who you are"; it's Todd finally telling his own student a thing he's sat on the whole time. The tablet on the table is what breaks it loose.

Todd goes still when he sees it. *"Where did you get that."* Then he tells him, because there's no reason left to hold it:

> "I never told you this, in all your years in my department. When I was a postgrad, my adviser took me to a house in Cambridge to meet an old man — **your grandfather,** your mother's father. He showed me a tablet over tea, and before I left he wrapped a small piece of it in cotton, put it in a cigar box, and asked me to **keep it for the family** until one of you came for it. I knew who you were the day you walked into my department, Bernard. I just never knew how to say it."

What he then reveals (grounded in canon):

> "Your great-grandfather was **B. R. Silvertongue.** The **Pamir** expedition. That piece in your hand is part of a tablet your family kept for a hundred years. There are **others.** And I have **one of them.**"

The Fragment III reveal + forward hook (`tablet_fragments.md:43–53`):

> "The piece itself is in São Carlos — the same cabinet as the tapes. It's **yours.** It always was. I'm only sorry it took the end of the world to make me give it to you."

And the bridge to Iris:

> "Two years ago I was told what that piece truly was, and that you would come for it. I've been waiting for your call since."

**Heritage, as much as the table wants** (deliver only canon — Pamir, Silvertongue, the four-fragment set, the Cambridge family archive of notebooks/translations; *leave Bernard's personal family detail to his player*). For Bernard, keep the beats simple and physical: *an old man, a drawer, a cigar box, "I've been waiting for you."*

> **Note on geography:** Fragment III is in **São Carlos,** not in Todd's pocket — he flew from Germany. So tonight delivers the **recognition + the knowledge + where the piece is,** not the physical handover. That's a future objective (and the cleaners are already moving on São Carlos — a clock on it).

### Q3 — "Who is hunting you, and why?"

His inference (correct, just unnamed):

> "Someone wants those cassettes gone and everyone who ever touched the programme quiet. I held thirteen years of their data and asked the wrong questions politely for twenty-five. I'm a loose end. So are you — you're just a loose end they think is already dead." *(He doesn't know how right that is.)*

### ▶ Iris's letters to Todd — why, and exactly what

**Letter 1 — late 2024 (Todd's marching orders).** *Single sheet, good paper, fountain pen, no letterhead, folded soft from two years in a drawer.*

> Professor Todd —
>
> I write on behalf of an old correspondence you were part of once, through people we both trusted and have both outlived.
>
> Sometime in the next year (or two) a call will come. The voice will be a stranger's, and then of someone you know. What they tell you will sound crazy. 
>
> When it comes: Go find them. Ignore men brandishing badges. Trust only the one that doesn't trust himself!
>
> Burn this if you like. You won't need this one.
>
> — *I.S.*

**Letter 2 — the one he was told he'd need.** Letter 1 signs off *"you won't need this one"* — because **this** is the one he would. Here's why Todd kept a two-year-old letter and carried it to this exact table:

**It is dated for the day of the meeting.** When Todd received it (over a year ago) the date on the page was still in the *future* — a letter dated for a day that hadn't happened yet. *That* impossible date is the whole hint. He kept it, unnerved, never quite able to throw it out; and the morning the meeting was set — for the very date written at the top — he understood the day had come, and brought it. It's functionally **addressed to the doctor**: Iris reaching two years forward through Todd to put a warning in Sally's hands. *(Drop in whatever date your meeting lands on — that's the only "date" in these docs worth pinning, and it pins itself. For a colder beat, keep it **sealed**: Todd hands Sally a letter written two years ago and dated today, and she breaks it open at the table.)*

> Professor —
>
> Still minding that homely little thing from Cambridge? Good. It has family it hasn't met yet — keep it off the internet (and the ARPAnet), and don't ask why; you'd only worry.
>
> And give your doctor my regards. Tell her not to keep anyone under observation for more than seven hours and thirty-nine minutes. And to watch her back.
>
> — *I.S.*


### ▶ The fake credentials (if a PC examines them)

A leather wallet, a convincing **[Bureau]** photo ID and badge for **"Special Agent R. Garrity."** Tells it's wrong (F2), free to a careful look: the office field is a real field office but the *area* doesn't match a Kallisto case; there's **no partner badge, no marked vehicle, no warrant, no procedure** — he's *herding,* not arresting; and a real agent doesn't make a witness vanish into an unmarked Suburban. Sean (ex-Chimera-overt, knows what real federal paper looks like) clocks it cold.

### ▶ The cassette Todd brought (physical object they leave with)

One tape he carried out of Germany in his jacket — the rest are in São Carlos. Grease-pencil code on the label: **`CRT-S/0427-19`**. It's the **anomalous one — Bernard's.**

**What's on it:** ~40 minutes of audio. A man's voice — **Bernard's** — calm, sedated, reciting in the dead Pamir tongue, fluent and unbroken, no interviewer audible. Under the voice, faint, a low steady tone: the CRT carrier, the **same 528 Hz Bernard now hums.**

**Todd's translation (folded into the case):** most is liturgy — invocation, repetition. Three lines he rendered clean, and they're why the tape matters:
> *"…the first one sleeps beneath the white waste and does not die… we are his hands and his voice… speak the name and he wakes…"*
And the recurring phrase he could never fully place: ***"ilū šū"*** — *"he is god."*

**Where it leads (for you, the DM):**
- It's a **Rosetta** — Bernard's fluent voice + Todd's notes = a working key to the **tablet's** language *and* to the chant from the shared dream.
- It describes **S-0** outright — *"the first one… beneath the white waste"* (Antarctica) — without anyone in the room yet knowing that's what it means.
- It's a gut-punch for Bernard: proof he's been speaking this **his whole life, asleep.** Play it once at the table and let it sit.

- **A keycard** (in Garrity's pocket) to the cleaners' **staging room in this same hotel** (e.g. **Room 305**). Inside:
  - A **surveillance file on Todd** — photos tracking him **São Carlos → Germany → here.** Proves they followed him from Brazil, and *how* they found him: a printout of **Kallisto's external-contractor list** with Todd's name ringed (the "loose end" made literal).
  - A **second printout** — a parallel tasking: *"SC SITE — RECOVERY/BURN — TEAM 2 INBOUND."* **A second team is hitting his São Carlos office.** That's the **clock on the archive + Fragment III** made concrete and urgent.
  - The **forged-credentials kit** — convincing federal ID stock and a laminator. Whoever runs these people has **state-level resources.** (Reads, correctly, as far above a lone fixer — Committee-tier — though no name is on any of it.)

None of it names the Committee. All of it **points:** Brazil is on a timer, someone with real reach wanted Todd dead, and they now have the PCs' faces.

## Where it leads

Out of the hotel with **Todd alive** and the first real **answers** of the campaign — the tapes, the dead tongue, the tablet, Iris's reach. Then the quiet knife: they **can't keep him.** Not because he's hurt — because of what happened to **Wider.** The field takes the better part of a day, but it's certain, and Iris's letter even named the hour. So Bernard gets his mentor back for one evening and has to **give him away** — to **Edmund** (the next thread) — while the **São Carlos** cabinet (the archive + Fragment III) is now a **race against a second team.** Pointed, finally, somewhere — and surer than ever that they can't go home, can't stay still, and **can't be near anyone they don't want to bury.**
