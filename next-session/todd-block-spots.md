# The block around the Holiday Inn — spots, interiors & occupants

*For when the PCs duck off-piste during (or instead of) the Todd job. Every adjacent building is keyed by letter to `todd-block.svg`. Each spot gives a quick **interior**, then three ready hooks: **🤡 a comic NPC**, **🎯 a serious NPC**, **⚠ a criminal-activity encounter.** None of these should swallow the Todd extraction — they're flavour, an exit, a complication, or a getaway, depending on what the table grabs. Philly row-house neighbourhood, late evening.*

---

## EAST

### PS — Parking Structure *(Renner's perch · across Main St, opposite the entrance)*
Concrete multi-level **across Main Street from the hotel front**, open-sided decks, fluorescent buzz and drips, oil-stained bays half-full of cars, an open stair tower, an attendant booth at the gate. Its open north face looks straight at the porte-cochère — which is *why* it's the perch.
- 🤡 **Pauly**, the gate attendant — a booth, a tiny TV, a hoagie, sports-talk radio. Waves cars through without looking up; wouldn't notice a body unless it landed on the ramp in front of him. *"Validated? …Whatever, go."*
- 🎯 **Renner himself** — parked at the open edge of an **upper deck**, elevated above the traffic, with a clean diagonal across Main Street to the entrance (binoculars + a phone on the rail). Cold, economical, on comms. He sees the *front*; he sees nothing of the back alley. The most dangerous man on the block to be caught by — at the entrance.
- ⚠ **Chop crew on L4** — two men stripping a stolen Audi after hours, impact wrenches whining, a lookout, pistols in waistbands. Territorial and twitchy; a running car and tools if you're bold — and a wildcard that can collide with Renner if the noise carries.

## SOUTH *(across Main Street)*

### D — The Silver Star *(24-hr diner)*
Chrome and cracked vinyl, coffee and griddle grease, a cook on the line, half a dozen night-owls, a window booth with a dead-on view of the hotel doors.
- 🤡 **Reggie**, sixth-coffee regular — wired, evangelising that the pigeons are surveillance drones, will "help" the instant anything kicks off and make it ten times worse. Loud, harmless, sticky.
- 🎯 **Doreen**, the night waitress, 50s — seen it all, misses nothing. Already clocked the four "feds" and the nervous professor across the street. Decent to her and she'll murmur a warning; trouble and she's thumbing 911 under the counter.
- ⚠ **Pill hand-off in the back booth** — two men, an envelope of oxy, jumpy and armed, a car idling in the rear lot. Spotted, they bolt or draw; the idling car is a getaway for anyone quick and ruthless.

### C — Kim's Cleaners *(closed)*
Steel grate down, dark showroom, a motorised conveyor of bagged clothes, a curtained back room. Locked.
- 🤡 **General**, the owner's parrot, left overnight on a perch behind the counter — shrieks *"TICKET PLEASE!"* and wolf-whistles at movement. A walking (squawking) noise trap for anyone sneaking in.
- 🎯 **Mr. Kim**, owner, asleep upstairs — down fast with a pump shotgun and a phone if the grate rattles. No fool, won't be sweet-talked, but reasonable if nobody's bleeding and they leave.
- ⚠ **Wash-house** — the back room launders cash for a local crew: a floor safe with banded bills and a betting ledger. The crew checks on it, so it's *watched*; cracking it = money and the exact wrong people's attention.

### T — The Townhouses *(row homes)*
Three-storey brick row, stoops, window AC units, an alley gap between two units; families and renters mostly asleep.
- 🤡 **Miss Antoinette**, 70s, "getting some air" on her stoop at midnight in a housecoat — gloriously nosy, narrates the whole street, will brief the PCs unbidden on every neighbour and all the "government cars" she's counted tonight.
- 🎯 **Marcus**, ER nurse just off a double, dead on his feet — the nearest real medic if there's a casualty. A moral pull and a problem: help, or keep him clear before the field (or a Stillpoint needle) makes him another body.
- ⚠ **Burglary in progress**, two doors down — a teenager jimmying a rear window. Spook-able into a hostage, a witness, or a quiet *"you didn't see me, I didn't see you."*

## NORTH *(behind the back alley)*

### B — Esquina 24h *(corner bodega/deli)*
Cramped, blazing fluorescent, beer and lottery and chips, bulletproof glass at the register, a grill in back, a bodega cat asleep on the chip rack.
- 🤡 **Tito**, night clerk, 19 — vaping behind the glass, thumbing a mobile shooter, narrates his kill-streak through anything, including gunfire outside. *"Yo that was crazy — slushie or nah?"*
- 🎯 **Doña Rosa**, owner, doing books in back, 60s — iron spine, a bat and a permitted pistol, hub of the block's informal mutual-aid. Knows everyone; would shelter a scared professor if asked right; cannot be intimidated.
- ⚠ **Fence in the back** — a steady trickle of "customers" not buying chips; a man moving stolen phones and laptops, cash in a fanny pack. Buys anything, asks nothing — or a strung-out stick-up about to go sideways at the register.

### L — Suds City *(24-hr laundromat)*
Warm, humid, detergent and lint, rows of front-loaders tumbling, a bolted-up TV running infomercials, a bill-changer, a couple of late patrons.
- 🤡 **Wendell**, retiree who launders at night "for the company" — crosswords, oversharing about his ex-wife, will absolutely rope a PC into 14-across mid-crisis.
- 🎯 **Aaliyah**, young mother folding hospital scrubs, a toddler asleep in a carrier on the table — pure innocence. If it turns loud anywhere near her, *that's* the gut-punch. Watches strangers warily.
- ⚠ **Dead drop** — a gym bag left in a specific dryer; a courier's coming for it (cash or product). Two parties, a handoff, and it curdles fast if interrupted. Or the bag's just sitting there, tumbling, tempting.

### A — The Walk-Up *(3-floor apartments)*
Broken buzzer panel, a vestibule that smells of weed and fried onions, a stairwell under a flickering hall light, thin doors with TVs murmuring behind them.
- 🤡 **"DJ Spinz"** (2F) — aspiring producer with a beat thumping at midnight, answers in headphones convinced the PCs are "here for the session," cannot be stopped talking about his SoundCloud.
- 🎯 **Mrs. Okafor** (3F) — a widow who keeps vigil by her window all night with tea and a phone. Sees *everything* in the alley below. Devout; will pray over a hurt man and call for help whether or not you want her to.
- ⚠ **Trap house** (1F) — a low-level dealer's flat: reinforced door, a peephole lookout, product and a gun inside, paranoia turned to eleven. Any knock reads as a stick-up or a raid. Bad to stumble into — but the lookout who sees the hotel commotion is a loose thread.

## WEST *(across the cross-street)*

### P — Liberty Loan & Pawn *(closed)*
Grated windows crammed with guitars, power tools, and rings; a dark showroom of glass cases; a steel-door back office with a safe; real cameras.
- 🤡 **Off-season animatronic Santa** — the owner never boxed it up; motion trips it and it lurches to life *"HO HO HO!"* in the black showroom. A genuine heart-stopper and a noise flare.
- 🎯 **Sol**, owner, lives above — ex-cop, 60s, comes down armed and unhurried, reads a room in a heartbeat. Will *deal* (information, gear, a quiet word, even a back-door exit) if there's profit and no mess — but he makes fugitives the second he sees them.
- ⚠ **Gun fencing out back** — a late buyer is collecting a pistol right now: cash, hardware, two men who'd rather stay invisible. A weapons source, or a deal you blunder into the middle of.

### V — The Anchor *(corner dive bar, open)*
Dark wood, neon beer signs, a long scarred bar, a pool table, a jukebox, a floor you stick to, and a handful of hard regulars.
- 🤡 **Big Eddie**, four beers past sense — *swears* he knows the PCs "from the thing, c'mon, the thing," buys a round, and becomes a loud, loyal liability who'll throw hands at anyone who bothers "his people."
- 🎯 **Frank**, the bartender — ex-merchant-marine, calm, a sawed-off racked under the taps. Runs a tight room, kills trouble before it starts, takes no side. His bar is genuinely off-limits for violence, and he means it.
- ⚠ **Back-room card game** — a bookie's table, serious cash, a couple of connected guys, a heavy on the door, and a cheating accusation about to boil. Walk in and you're a player, a thief, or a cop — none of them good.

### R — West Row + Storefront Church
Another quiet residential row under a flickering streetlight; parked cars; a small storefront **church** at the corner with its lights still on.
- 🤡 **Stan**, in a bathrobe, waging nightly war on a raccoon raiding his trash — deadly earnest about "the operation," will conscript any passerby into the ambush.
- 🎯 **Pastor Lewis** — up late, doors unlocked for anyone who needs them. A true sanctuary; he'd take in a frightened man and ask nothing. *(Quiet catch: he's a helix-negative in a closed room with the PCs — the 7:39 field clock starts the moment they shelter there.)*
- ⚠ **Stolen-car swap** — a man under the corner streetlight changing plates on a sedan, keys in the ignition, engine warm. A ready getaway for the bold — or a jittery witness who just watched the PCs walk out of the hotel.

## DM notes
- **Getaway cars** are seeded on purpose (D's rear lot, PS's chop job, R's plate-swap) — if the players torch or ditch the rednecks' truck, a replacement is never far.
- **Witnesses** are everywhere (Doreen, Miss Antoinette, Mrs. Okafor, the streamer #14 inside) — the "stayed invisible" win means managing *people*, not just cameras.
- **Sanctuary with a price:** the church (R) and Doña Rosa (B) are the two spots that'd genuinely take Todd in — but a helix-negative sheltering them runs the field clock, and a kind stranger who helps tonight is a body in a week.
- Keep each of these to a *scene*, not a *session*. If the table sinks into the card game or the trap house, the Todd clock (the cleaners moving) is your lever to pull them back.
