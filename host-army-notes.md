## Off-books IMHO

- **2013, central Asia — extraction.** Two-man team. **Kesłowski lead, second man unknown to me.** They both came back rattled. Both stopped talking about it after a month. Country I'm guessing — could be Pakistan, could be Afghanistan, could be further north.
- **2015, Russia — research lab penetration.** Four-man. Don't have names. Heard about it third-hand from a man who'd been on overwatch.
- **2018, Germany — single-operator removal.** Made to look like suicide. **I think Reyes was the trigger** — could be wrong, I'm assuming because of the timing of his transfer. Whoever ran it would have had outside cover; that part I never heard about.
- **2019, Japan — lab theft.** **Horowitz ran comms, near-certain.** Other names not known to me.
- **2020, Greece — long surveillance.** I have nothing solid on this one. Heard one of the operators came back with a long beard and wouldn't say where.
- **2022, Brazil — payoff or quiet enforcement, not sure.** Two-man. Names gone.
- **2023, Boston — pressure on a reporter.** *I don't actually know if this was a Serrat op or a parallel network. Including it because the timing fits.*
- **2024, Switzerland — counter-surveillance somewhere.** I have almost nothing here. Could be misremembered as one of the earlier ones.
- **2025, eastern Europe — the one that wiped half of ODA-3223.** The team went in heavy and most of them didn't come out. Two survivors that I know of. One never spoke again (Aragones). The other is probably Burton?

**Pattern across all of them:** small teams. Always small. Mixed across ODAs — he'd pick a medic from one team, a comms from another, a weapons from a third. No single team ever saw the whole op. That's why nobody talked: nobody had the whole story.

The exception is 2025. That one was a full team. How did they fuck it up? I don't know what was different about that one.

## What I'd swear is wrong but can't prove

There must have been somebody above Serrat too. A Colonel running a Special Forces Group is senior, but his orders still get reviewed, his promotions still get vetted, his classified taskings still pass through SOCOM staff. Foreign extractions, kills made to look like accidents, off-the-books operator pipelines for fifteen years — none of that survives an inspector general for that long without a one-star or higher quietly running interference. **There's a name I won't write down** because I might be wrong and I don't want to disrespect a dead man. If somebody asks me out loud I might say.
