# Layer 1 — The Halder Foundation

## What it is publicly

A mid-sized old-money biomedical foundation, founded 1962 by Dr. Everett Halder. Public profile: philanthropic biomedical research, antiviral programmes, public-health initiatives. Files Form 990s. Operates a respectable board. Funds academic research at universities including Halder Genomics Initiative at Boston University.

## What it actually is

**The respectability layer.** The Foundation exists to give everything underneath the legitimacy that a private corporation and a classified federal programme cannot generate on their own. Foundation grants pay for legitimate work, cover for less legitimate work, and route money where the structure beneath needs it.

Most employees — the philanthropy office, the grants administration team, the public-health partners, the legal counsel — work on actual philanthropy and know nothing about what sits under them.

## Real assets

- **The Boston campus parcel.** Owned by the Foundation since 1962. Now glass.
- **Halder Mansion.** Family estate east of the campus, outside the strike radius. Holds the pre-1962 archive vault (Halder's expedition logs, Wittenbach translations, helix-positive register cards, Halder's last working drafts). Intact, currently empty, under quiet surveillance.
- **Foundation endowment.** Approximately $480 million as of last public filing. Currently under partial federal asset-freeze pending investigation.
- **Halder Genomics Initiative** (Boston University), a Foundation-funded antiviral lab run by Edmund.
- A foreign-charitable arm registered in Germany, sometimes used as a routing vehicle for Frankfurt-side payments.

## Key people

- **Edmund Halder** (Board Chair, b. 1973). Public, on television, publicly campaigning against the Committee. Has begun releasing Foundation records. Helix-positive without knowing how. Single most dangerous public figure to the Committee right now.
- **The Foundation Board** — eight members, most of whom have been told the cover story. Three are Committee-aligned and have been quietly resisting Edmund's disclosure push.
- **Foundation legal counsel** — partly controlled by Klaus through long-standing relationships. Currently fighting any federal warrant for Mansion access.
- **The philanthropy office staff** — sincere, oblivious, working through grants schedules.

### Historical / founding-era (all deceased)

- **Dr. Everett Halder** (1919–1984). The founder. 1939 Pamir expedition; built the institutions 1945–1962. Held the Committee's founding seat 1962–1984. Poisoned in March 1984 on Klaus's vote, via Geneva.
- **J. Halder II** (1942–2018). Edmund's father. Held the Board Chair 1984–2018; given a quiet inherited consultative role on the Committee but deliberately kept naive. Never knew what was being done in his father's name.
- **Dr. Lenore Aspe** (1928–2003). Halder's clinical right hand from 1962 until her death. Ran the residence-wing medicine without ever joining Vogt's parallel track.
- **Dr. Lionel West** (1922–1967). Halder's founding biochemist. Killed by his own synthesis in the Old Laboratory.
- **The seven men of the 1939 Pamir expedition.** Halder's first cut. All dead by the late 1970s; four of them not naturally.

## Current state post-strike

- Edmund has gone public, called for "independent Congressional inquiry above any classification ceiling," named Klaus Esterhazy on Day 3.
- Internal Board faction war — Edmund versus Committee-aligned members — currently quiet but coalescing.
- Federal investigators have access to all public Foundation records and to whatever Edmund has voluntarily released. They do not yet have access to the Mansion archive or to the deepest financial routing.
- The Foundation legal counsel is running protective interference on the Committee's behalf without Edmund's authorisation — a tension that will surface within weeks.

## Tied to

- Kallisto (parent → subsidiary)
- Chimera (provides legitimate cover for Chimera funding)
- The Committee (Klaus has long-standing relationships with three Board members and the legal counsel)
- Edmund as an outlier inside this layer is the wedge into all of it

A private biotech corporation, spun out of the Halder Foundation's applied-research arm in 1977 by Gideon March. Public face: federally-contracted biomedical research and applied biogenesis. # Layer 3 — Project Chimera

## What it is

A classified US government programme, founded 1962 by Halder under medical-intelligence contracting. Funded by federal black budget routed through DoD and DoE accounts. Classification above SECRET; specifics above TOP SECRET on the covert side.

Chimera does not have employees in the usual sense. Its personnel are seconded from other agencies or are nominally Kallisto / Foundation employees with classified clearances. Its budgets are line items in larger agency appropriations. Its existence has been a classified fact for sixty-four years.

## Internal structure — the two compartments

**Director: Sean** (currently presumed dead). Acting director: **Dr. Adam Strauss** (Committee's man, appointed within 48 hours of the strike).

The overt compartment funds and oversees:

- The antiviral programme run by **Henrik Loew** as Programme Director. Real antiviral work, real publications, real federal value.

- Cognitive-resonance and neurocognitive research (the CRT programme; Patel was Kallisto's lead until his death in the strike; Todd at USP São Carlos was a long-term overt-compartment consultant).

**This is real work.** Loew has spent forty-two years protecting Halder's original mission inside this compartment. The overt compartment's papers, grants, and personnel are largely defensible if examined.

Sean signed the budget instruments for Chimera as a whole, which included covert allocations he did not see in detail.

**Director: Gideon March** (currently off-grid).

# Above all three — The Committee

## What it is

A small body of patrons-of-record and senior government insiders. Not a legal entity. Has no offices, no website, no acknowledged existence. Approximately a dozen members. The Committee predates Chimera (Esterhazy patronage going back to the 1900s) and was formalised as the strategic oversight body when Halder founded Chimera in 1962.

## What it actually does

**Strategic ownership without operational footprint.** The Committee does not execute anything. It approves directions, provides political and financial cover, decides at the top who lives and who dies on a strategic scale (it voted to kill Halder in 1984; it will eventually vote on Gideon, Strauss, Serrat, and Edmund).

The Committee's mechanism is **leverage and patronage.** Members hold seats on the boards of relevant corporations, hold senior positions in relevant government bodies, hold senior positions in adjacent foreign institutions. When Chimera needs a federal contract granted, a Committee member at the relevant agency arranges it. When a foreign government needs to be told something, a Committee member with relevant relationships tells them. When a journalist needs to be silenced, a Committee member with relevant assets makes that happen.

## Known members

- **Klaus Esterhazy** (Vienna, head of Committee). Inherited his mother Marian's seat in 1975. Has been the operational head since approximately 1980. Currently named publicly by Edmund Halder. **Locked all known social media accounts within 90 minutes of being named.**
- **Two other current seats** — open at the table, not yet locked. The Committee currently has ~12 members; nine are unnamed in canon.

## Historic members and operatives (all deceased)

- **Marian Esterhazy** (1908–1975). Klaus's mother. Committee patron, Halder's funding line from 1962. Inherited the seat to Klaus on her death.
- **Col. Byron Archer** (1924–1979). Halder's federal liaison and founding-era Committee seat for operations. Killed by Hadj when his disillusionment threatened the paediatric trial.
- **Dr. Everett Halder** (1919–1984). Founding seat, removed when his protective programme threatened the Committee's strategic project. Killed on the Committee's vote.
- **Dr. Hannes Vogt** (1942–2014). Committee operative placed inside Building B 1978–1985 to run the parallel forced-expression track Halder would not authorise.
- **Geneva Loomis** (1947–2018). Iris's childhood tutor, arrived at the household in 1974. The Committee's quiet operative inside the family. Poisoned Halder in March 1984. Disappeared from the institution in 1995; lived under cover; died of natural causes in 2018.

# Serrat's private network

## What it is

A semi-independent operational layer **inside** Kallisto's covert compartment. Approximately twelve contractors under Serrat's direct control, plus the operators who served under him in 18D/18A and went on his foreign missions.

Distinguished from the Committee's other resources because Serrat **owns these people personally.** Their loyalty is to him, not to Klaus or the Committee. The Frankfurt route runs through them. The foreign missions ran through them. The current cleanup of the operator chain is running through them.

## What the network actually served (1995–2026)

Not a clean either/or. The pipeline was **Kallisto-tasked, Serrat-skimmed.** Klaus and the covert compartment used the network as their expeditor arm — sample and equipment thefts from competing labs, extractions of Wittenbach-line carriers from Halder's 1976 register, removals of journalists and witnesses who came too close to the cover-floor seam, surveillance ops Klaus did not want routed through formal contractors. Kallisto paid in three currencies: money through the Frankfurt-adjacent shells, protection (clean federal records, accelerated promotions, the Chimera-issued field medkits), and information — which is Serrat's actual currency.

On every operation Serrat kept a piece back: a courier manifest, a sample residue, a photograph that wasn't supposed to leave the site, a cross-referenced name. He doesn't trust his patrons and never has. Over fifteen years he assembled **a private file on Kallisto's covert compartment itself** — what Iris was actually doing on B3, who paid for the 1979 paediatric trial, the Sokol material, the names of every Committee principal who ever signed an authorisation. The file is his insurance against Klaus turning on him and his retirement instrument if he ever has to sell. Mironov is the buyer he has been cultivating for that contingency. The file's physical location is one of the three private vaults (Geneva / Liechtenstein / the third) — which one is open for the table to discover.

If she surfaces alive in the coming weeks, the calculus flips fast — capture-preferred while Ron can still be wrung out of her, kill-if-forced if she's about to become a federal witness.

# The Listeners

## What they are

A helix-religious community in the Pindos mountains of northern Greece, founded by Anselm Yates in 2004. Roughly 280 people in the enclave; ~120 are helix-positive descendants Yates has recruited, ~80 are original villagers indifferent to the cult, ~60 are inner Listeners (the dangerous core), ~20 are professionals supporting the community.

## Doctrine

The helix gene is sacred inheritance from S-0. Awakening is approached as a sacrament. Kallisto's harvesting of S-0 is sacrilege. The Listeners are kin to all helix-positive carriers and consider it a duty to bring them into the community.

## Key people

- **Anselm Yates** (founder, b. 1963). Quiet, methodical, theologically rigorous. Has not been on Kallisto premises since 1998. Currently in the Pindos enclave reading the news obsessively. **Believes Iris is dead.** **Does not yet know the PCs survived.**

- **The 60 inner Listeners** — devotional, often slipping into mutation, sometimes dangerous in ways that don't translate to a battlefield.

# How the layers actually interact in practice

A single example: **the Tajik villager extraction in 2013.**

- **The Committee** (Klaus, then his late 30s and consolidating power) decided that Halder's pre-1976 register needed to be operationalised — descendants Halder had refused to bring to Boston should be brought now, by force if necessary.
- **Chimera's covert compartment** (under Gideon as Director) authorised the operation.
- **Kallisto** provided the corporate cover — a forged "medical NGO" identity, a real biotech-staffed receiving point in Tbilisi, a stasis crate from the Antarctic supply chain.
- **The Halder Foundation** funded the operation through a grant labelled "international rare-disease outreach," which paid the operators and the Tbilisi infrastructure.
- **Serrat** (then still Col. Serrat openly, pre-Vance cover) ran the operation through his 18D channels. Two operators on the ground for the extraction itself.
- **The villager** was taken to Tbilisi, transferred to Boston, held in B2, forced into expression, and died in 2015.

That is one operation. The Foundation paid for it. The Foundation board did not know. Kallisto staffed it. Most Kallisto employees did not know. Chimera classified it. Sean was Director of the overt compartment at the time and signed budget that included it without seeing the line. The Committee owned it strategically. Klaus would have known the broad strokes.

**That pattern, multiplied across thirty-plus operations and forty years, is the structure.**
