# Encounter A — The airfield

## Setup

The contact drives them to a private grass strip in rural southern Maine. One hangar, one fuel pump, one **Beechcraft King Air 350ER** (twin turboprop, ~3,000 nm range with the extended tanks the pilot has retrofitted, eight passenger seats stripped to four plus cargo). **Pilot** — the contact's old buddy, ex-Air Force, runs unregistered cargo for cash. **Realistic route:** Maine private strip → Lajes (Azores) for refuel → Dakar (Senegal) for refuel → Cape Town. About 36 hours total including ground time. Bench seats, earplugs, no questions.

## The complication

Serrat's cleanup team got there first. **The contact, not the PCs, is the primary target** — Serrat knows the contact has been a friend of several men on her kill list and assumes he is the next obvious step in her cleanup. The operators followed his vehicle through a fuel-stop trace. **Three operators on the ground, one marksman on a low ridge 400 m east with a thermal scope.** They have been in position for two hours when the PCs arrive at ~18:30. They are not expecting the four PCs at all. **Serrat believes the PCs died in the strike.**

When the operators see Sally and the Killer dismount the contact's pickup, they radio it in. There is a forty-second pause before contact orders come back: *"Confirm. Kill all. Confirm again on extract."* The kill order is now general.

## Tactical situation

- The hangar is lit; the pilot is visible doing pre-flight.
- Tyre tracks in the gravel don't match the contact's prior visits (Perception DC 15).
- Treeline runs along the north side of the strip — usable cover.
- The marksman's ridge has a clean line on the strip and the hangar interior.
- The King Air takes nine minutes to start, taxi, and lift off.

# Encounter B — The cabin

## Setup

**04:00. No moon. Light snow falling at ~1 cm/hour.** Air temperature −15°C. The cabin generator failed earlier in the evening; the place is heated only by the wood stove and is cold within a few metres of the walls.

The cabin sits in a small clearing of compacted snow, about thirty metres across. Trees press in on all sides. The only road in is a single-track dirt path running south-west.

**Gus**, the host's husky-mix (5 years old, ~30 HP, intelligent, trained), was outside relieving himself when something in the east treeline made him freeze. He returned to the cabin walking sideways with his hackles up. The host clocked it instantly.

**The PCs have ~90 seconds of warning** between Gus's behaviour and the first shot.

## The complication

Serrat's team is coming **for the cabin host, not the PCs.** Serrat traced the host's last fuel-card hit on 14 April (Lebanon NH, northbound). Her people have been working backward through known properties he might own. They located this cabin yesterday and put it under satellite-imagery surveillance.

Their brief: cabin host alive if possible for interrogation, dead if not. **They do not know the PCs are with him.** Serrat believes the PCs died in the strike.

When the operators see five silhouettes through their thermals instead of one, the team leader (Hatch) takes thirty seconds to call it in. The return order: *"confirm all four, kill any who match, leave host last."* That's when the engagement starts.

## In-cabin objects

- **Host's desk drawer:** the **journal** (two months of tracked deaths). Host knows where it is; he'll grab it on his way out if alive.
- **Cooler on the counter:** the **two vials of airborne-conversion agent** the PCs brought from the warehouse. **If the cooler is struck by fire or explosion damage, the seal fails — 3d10 necrotic to all helix-negatives in a 30 ft radius, PCs unaffected.** Useable as a weapon if released deliberately.
- The vials' magnetic-latch carrying case can be grabbed and run with.

## The cold

- Non-PC creatures outside without proper gear or shelter take one level of exhaustion per 30 minutes. The host and operators have gear. The PCs have warehouse gear.
- Wounded characters (below half HP) bleeding in the snow take **1 cold damage per round** outside until stabilised.
- Shouting at this temperature produces visible steam. The Marksman uses this.

## Hostiles

### Hatch — Lieutenant (CR 6, action-oriented boss)

**Position: at the edge of the eastern treeline, ~25 m (80 ft) from the cabin's east wall, behind a thick pine.** He directs his three operators forward from there. He moves to a second tree if his cover becomes untenable. He does not advance to the cabin himself — he is the command node, not the line.

- **AC** 18 (kevlar IIIA + plate carrier insert), **HP** 110, **Speed** 30 ft
- **STR** 16, **DEX** 16 (+3), **CON** 16 (+3), **INT** 14 (+2), **WIS** 14 (+2), **CHA** 12
- **Saves** Str +6, Dex +6, Con +6, Wis +5
- **Multiattack** 3 (2× M4 + 1× sidearm, or 3× M4).
- **M4:** +6, range 80/240, 2d8+3 piercing (12).
- **Sidearm:** +6, 1d10+3 piercing (8).
- **Combat Veteran.** Advantage on saves vs frightened, charmed, surprised.
- **Tactical Eye (reaction).** When an ally within 30 ft is hit by a critical, Hatch can redirect — original target takes half, Hatch takes half.

#### Bonus actions (one per round)

- **Barked Command.** One operator within 30 ft makes an M4 attack with advantage.
- **Take Cover.** Hatch gains three-quarters cover until end of next turn.
- **Suppressing Fire.** One M4 attack at –5; target's speed halved until end of next turn.

#### Lair-style escalations (free, triggered)

- **Round 3** (or earlier if 2+ operators are down, or any PC tries to leave the clearing): Hatch uses his bonus action to **transmit on his collar mic.** *"Roost, Three. Wedge active. Send."* — the call to launch the helicopter. **The call takes Hatch's full bonus action that round. If interrupted, the call fails.**
- **Round 5** (if the call went out on Round 3): the helicopter arrives. See Encounter C escalation.

#### How the PCs detect the radio call

Two signals; either is enough.

1. **Visible motion.** Any PC with line of sight to Hatch's tree (east window, roof, woodpile facing east) sees him **drop his rifle muzzle, free his left hand, and reach up to press his collar mic.** Perception DC 12 to recognise it as a radio transmission given he's also been firing.
2. **Bernard's supernatural hearing.** At 80 ft Hatch's collar-mic speech is just audible to gene-amplified hearing. **Passive Perception 14+ catches it automatically with advantage from supernatural hearing**; otherwise active DC 12. Bernard hears the words: *"Roost, Three. Wedge active. Send."*

Either signal triggers at **the start of Hatch's turn on Round 3.** The call resolves at end of his turn. Same-round interruption works.

#### Radio specifically

The radio is on Hatch's chest rig. Destroying it specifically: called shot, AC 19 (disadvantage), 25 HP. Sean's Eldritch Blast can target it. A successful hit of 15+ damage to Hatch during the call disrupts the transmission.

### Marksman — Ridge sniper (CR 5)

**Position: 220 m east on a low ridge, prone behind a fallen pine. Thermal scope. Suppressed bolt-action.**

Same stat block as Encounter A. First shot is Sneak Attack from concealment. **Repositions every two rounds** to maintain line of sight.

**He is outside everyone's spell range.** Cutting Words, Faerie Fire, Eldritch Blast, Hypnotic Pattern — none reaches him. The party deals with him by:

- **Killer Wild Shape to Giant Eagle** (Druid 5 max CR 1; Giant Eagle is CR 1, fly 80 ft). On the ridge in two rounds. Direct engagement. **Roll the Tyrant trigger when Wild Shaping.**

- **Concealment via terrain.** A character behind the woodpile or inside the cabin is not a viable target.

He will not advance. If everyone else is dead, he fades into the woods — he has a snowmobile staged 400 m further east.

## Multiple objectives

The PCs are not just trying to survive. Each is a separate possible outcome.

1. **Survive.**
2. **Stop Hatch from calling the helicopter.** Kill Hatch before Round 3, destroy his radio, drop **Silence** on his position (Bernard's load-bearing move — 120 ft range, reaches him easily), or interrupt with a successful 15+ damage hit during the call.
3. **Keep the host alive.** He will fight (Veteran CR 3, hunting rifle). His journal is in his desk drawer.
4. **Keep Gus alive.** Doesn't change the world. Changes the tone.
5. **Don't let any operator escape with intel.** Diaz running for the truck is the biggest risk.
6. **Capture an operator alive.** Diaz is the easy one. The Marksman is the valuable one.
7. **Recover the vials before the cooler is struck.** If the cabin burns, the cooler ruptures and either kills helix-negatives in 30 ft (operators included) or vents into snow uselessly.
8. **Preserve the truck.** They may need it to leave fast.
9. **Preserve the sat-dish.** If they want to make outside calls before decamping, it must survive.

## Phases

### Phase 1 — Engagement (Rounds 1–2)

- **Round 1, initiative 20:** the Marksman fires unseen at the most exposed PC. Sneak Attack damage. The shot's source is locatable only after the second shot.
- **Round 1, regular initiative:** the three Operators close to the treeline edge (~30 m / 100 ft from the cabin), open fire, take cover.
- **Round 1 priority target:** the sat-dish (one Operator fires at it).
- **Round 2:** Operators advance in pairs. Diaz throws his flashbang at whichever PCs have clustered (or the cabin doorway).

### Phase 2 — Pressure (Rounds 3–4)

- **Round 3:** Hatch attempts the radio call. PCs with line of sight see the motion; Bernard hears the words. **Stopping the call is the most important reactive moment of the fight.**
- **Round 3–4:** Operators commit to closing on the cabin or break and try to take it room by room. Diaz may make for the truck.
- **Round 3–4:** Marksman repositions to a new ridge (loses one round of shooting, gains line on previously-concealed target).

### Phase 3 — Resolution or Escalation (Round 5+)

- **If the radio call did not go out:** the fight winds down. Operators dead, surrendering, or fled. Marksman on his snowmobile.
- **If the call went out:** the helicopter arrives at Round 5. See Encounter C escalation.

# Encounter C — Helicopter escalation

## Setup

A Little Bird helicopter (private contractor, ex-military airframe sold on the grey market) arrives as Serrat's escalation. Can be triggered from either Encounter A or Encounter B if the ground team fails to neutralise the PCs, or as its own standalone scene later.

## How it slots in

- **As escalation from A:** the heli is 30 minutes out from a Maine private field when the airfield fight starts. If the ground team has not confirmed kills by the time the Cessna is taxiing for departure, the heli arrives overhead. Now the PCs are dealing with takeoff under suppressing fire.
- **As escalation from B:** the heli arrives 45–60 minutes after the cabin firefight. Catches the PCs only if they linger to patch wounds. The forest gives them a 60–70% survival probability if they break for the treeline immediately.
- **As its own scene:** Serrat skips the ground team and comes in with the heli directly, at a location the PCs are exposed (a road, an open field, a parking area).

## Tactical situation

- The heli hears before it sees. *Whup-whup-whup* over the treeline at low altitude, then it's on top of them.
- Spotlight + thermal optic + door minigun. Pilot in the cockpit, gunner in the open hatch.
- The heli cannot hover lower than 50 ft above the highest local obstacle. **Under heavy treecover, under building overhangs, in a steep ravine — the heli cannot follow.**
- Heli has ~2-3 hours of station time before it has to refuel.

## Hostile

- **Little Bird (private contractor):** stat block above in earlier design. AC 17/14/11 depending on facing. HP 220. Speed fly 120 ft hover / 400 ft advance. Crew (counted separately): **Pilot (Veteran CR 3)** and **Door Gunner (Veteran CR 3)** with M134 minigun.

**Encounter XP:** vehicle CR 10 (5,900) + 2× CR 3 (1,400) = 7,300 raw, ~14,600 adjusted. **Deadly** but environmentally manageable — cover wins.

## Outcomes

- **Pilot down:** the heli autorotates into the trees. Crash, fireball. 4d10 falling damage to anything within 30 ft of the crash site.
- **Pilot survives, heli withdraws:** Serrat knows the PCs survived the ground team. Next contact will be heavier.
- **Heli grounded intact (unlikely):** the tail number leads somewhere with effort.

## Loot

### If the heli crashes

  1. Photograph or copy the tail number.
  2. The FAA Aircraft Registry is a public, free, online database (registry.faa.gov). Anyone with internet can search N409WX.
  3. The search returns the registered owner: **West Mountain Holdings LLC, 1712 Pioneer Ave, Cheyenne WY** — same shell as the Ford Expedition from Encounter B's I-93 staging point. The address is a registered-agent service that fronts hundreds of similar shells.
  4. Wyoming LLCs do not require beneficial-owner disclosure in public records. **The PCs cannot get past the shell themselves.** Edmund Halder or Evelyn Ross can pursue it — Edmund through Foundation legal counsel (three weeks of audit work, traces the shell back through three layers to a Cayman holding company), Ross through CIA-side financial-intelligence channels (faster, but requires her to formally open a file).
  5. **The shell connects to the Encounter B Expedition and the Manchester safehouse — same financial layer.** So whatever the PCs recover from the heli reinforces what they already have from B.

- The door gunner's body, ejected on impact, has a **sat phone** in his vest. The phone has one stored contact entry: **"L"**, a +41 22 Geneva landline. **What the PCs can do with this:** dial it. # The Serrat arc

The next two to four sessions sit inside what's effectively the Serrat hunt. The PCs are her targets; she is also their best chance to bring federal investigators down on the Committee's logistical layer. The arc resolves when she is dead, captured, or extracted to a state that won't extradite her.

## Serrat's motives, in layers

She is not one-dimensional. Six things drive her at once and they cohere into a coherent person.

1. **Survival.** Federal indictments on the Munich removal alone would mean Supermax for life. Several other mission files would support capital prosecution. She has to bury the evidence chain before the federal investigation reaches it.
2. **Wealth.** She has roughly $2–3 million in portable assets across **three private vaults** — Geneva (cash and bearer bonds), Liechtenstein (art lifted from extraction targets, including two pre-Soviet icons taken from the Tajik villager's family in 2013), and a third holding she has not told anyone about. Federal asset-freezing on Kallisto-adjacent accounts has not reached any of these yet because they are personal, not Foundation-routed.
3. **Ideology.** She genuinely believes the airborne-conversion programme was a strategic national asset wasted by the Committee on humanitarian caution. She thinks Klaus Esterhazy is a coward. She would happily restart the work — and intends to — under a state she views as serious. Russia is her primary candidate. **Mironov is her exit option** if she can negotiate the right terms.
4. **Pride.** Fifteen years of operational craft. She will not accept disgrace. She will fight rather than flee until the option is run-or-die.
5. **Leverage against Klaus Esterhazy** (current Committee head in Vienna, who has been Serrat's nominal Committee-side principal since 2019). She has a small **private archive** of pre-1984 Halder materials she took out of Building B over years — Aspe notebook pages, Halder's 1977 internal memo on Sokol, a 1982 letter Halder wrote that names two Committee members from the period. She holds this as insurance against Klaus. **The relationship is currently fraying** because Klaus knows the federal investigation will eventually reach her, and is calculating whether to dispose of her himself before she has to make a deal.
6. **Sally as a special case.** Per canon, Sally was Serrat's 18D subordinate and was recruited by Evelyn Ross's CIA cell to scout Kallisto's foreign-sales pipeline. Serrat had reasons to suspect Sally as an undeclared CIA presence but never moved against her — moving against an active CIA asset would have surfaced operational exposure she could not afford. **The strike resolved that constraint for Serrat.** Sally was crossed out as presumed dead. If the encounters reveal Sally is alive, Serrat moves to active hunt immediately. **What in Sally's history made her a CIA recruit, what she has done under that recruitment, and what she knows about Serrat's operations is Sally's player's to lock.**

- **The Serrat clock is real.** Every hour Sally spends investigating is an hour Serrat's parallel investigation also runs. If Sally takes too long, Scene C becomes the default.
- **If Ron lives,** he is **not** a permanent party member. He has his own life, his own threat, his own decisions to make. He travels with the PCs for the back end of the Serrat arc and then his arc closes — either he goes into deep cover for good, or he turns federal witness against Serrat with Sally's contact at the CIA cell, or he dies in a later encounter, or whatever the player wants.
