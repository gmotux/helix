Timeline anchor:

- **April 14, 14:23** — rupture (Iris breaches the containers; mist floods the Kallisto Boston campus). No outward signs; the building goes quiet from outside.
- **April 14–17** — the rupture-and-cordon crisis. The public sees an unexplained federal cordon around Kallisto's campus, an aggressive no-fly zone, an evacuation. No nuclear event yet.

The four PCs are inside the campus / the cavern / the kitchen / the tunnel for the entire window. They have no access to news. This document captures what they would have seen.

For each window, three layers:
- **OFFICIAL** — government press releases, official statements, executive remarks
- **PRESS** — mainstream and credible outlet reporting
- **WILD** — open-source intelligence threads, social media, conspiracy ecosystems, foreign media

Source-realism rules used throughout:
- The campus is publicly known to be **Kallisto Biomedical's Boston facility**, owned by the Halder Foundation. A single property-records search reveals this. The federal cover stories work *with* this fact, not against it.
- **Project Chimera** is classified above the threshold reporters can publicly discuss until a specific leak surfaces the name. The first leak is on Day 0 evening (LinkedIn profile, a discharged employee's record); from then on, the name is in the discourse.
- Pre-strike, there is **no nuclear event** — no seismic signature, no CTBTO activation, no international military monitoring. Russian Telegram analysts only activate at T+0 of the strike.
- Pre-strike, there is also **no public biological framing**. CDC and USAMRIID are not named in press. The federal silence is the story.

# THE STRIKE (April 17, 14:00 EDT)

The post-strike windows continue from T+0, with T+0 = the strike at ~14:00 EDT on April 17, 2026.

The post-strike cover story crystallises around a single line: **"Kallisto Biomedical was operating under a classified federal contract that involved fissile research material. The material reached prompt criticality. Federal authorities authorised extraordinary measures to ensure containment."**

The story works for one news cycle. It works because:
- It acknowledges Kallisto owns the site (the public already knows this).
- It explains why the federal response was so opaque (classification).
- It blames "extraordinary measures" without saying *nuclear weapon* until forced.

The story fails inside 24 hours because:
- International seismic monitoring (CTBTO, USGS, Norwegian/Swedish/Japanese stations) immediately publishes that the signature is a fission weapon, not a criticality event.
- Kallisto holds no NRC licence for fissile material (reporters check this on Day 0 evening).
- Russia, China, EU all publicly demand explanations within 12 hours.

By Day 1 the official line evolves into: *"Kallisto's federally-contracted work involved materials whose nature remains classified. Federal authorities used a tactical demolition device. The decision was the President's. The American people will receive a full accounting in due course."* This admits the nuclear character without admitting what it was sterilising.
