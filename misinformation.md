## The arc (the cover story, and how it breaks)

1. **Silence (Apr 14–17).** A huge cordon + 100-mile no-fly + evacuation, no explanation. The press reports *about* the response; the internet seethes; nobody has a verified fact.
2. **The strike + the cover (Apr 17).** Official line: *"Kallisto was under a classified federal contract involving fissile material; it reached prompt criticality; extraordinary containment measures were authorised."* Holds one news cycle.
3. **The cover breaks (Apr 17–18).** International seismic monitoring says **fission weapon, not criticality.** Kallisto holds **no fissile licence.** Russia / China / the EU demand answers. The line retreats to *"a tactical device, the President's decision, a full accounting in due course"* — admitting the nuke without admitting what it sterilised.
4. **The leaks (Apr 17–18).** A deleted LinkedIn surfaces **"Project Chimera"** → **#WhatIsChimera.** The **named-missing list** of summit attendees goes up. Conspiracy threads stumble toward the residence wing, the 1979 paediatric trial, even **"S-0," "the flower," "four who walked out"** — all dismissed as crackpot.
5. **Edmund (Apr 18 → 21).** **Edmund Halder** goes public, releases Foundation records, calls for an inquiry "above any classification ceiling," and on Day 4 **names Klaus Esterhazy.** The cover collapses; the story shifts to *"who's been running classified bio-research under medical cover since 1962?"*
