# Ron Bennett — Sally's Case (personal arc, player-driven)

Sally's thread, not an evidence hunt. Low-key by default; it runs at her pace and only if she pulls on it.

## Who he is / current status

- **SFC Ron Bennett, 18D medic, ODA-3223** — Sally's own ODA. **NOT** on the 2025 Moldova op (detached stateside), so he survived where most of the team died.
- **Off-grid for about a month** — he went dark a few weeks before the rupture, around the same time Wider started his journal. The same disappearance pattern that spooked Wider spooked Ron; he moved first.
- He's been **tracking the killings in parallel to Wider** — building his own list. He keeps an **operational journal** of the foreign missions he was actually on.
- On Serrat's kill ledger he *was* ACTIVE — HIGHEST PRIORITY (alongside Wider).

## Redesign caveat (read this first)

- **Serrat dropped the hunt.** Post-cabin he's running, not killing. So Ron is **no longer being actively chased** — his immediate danger has receded, and the most consistent default now is **Ron is alive and successfully hidden.** Serrat never got him and now never will.
- **Sally already knows he's missing** — she read it in Wider's recovered list. So the inciting beat is *done.* This file is **how she actually finds him,** not how she learns he's gone.
- **Urgency is low.** This is a slow, personal arc Sally can pursue between bigger fires. Don't put a clock on it unless you want one (see the federal wrinkle below).

## The hard guardrail

**Do not write Sally's history with Ron.** `factions.md` establishes he's her partner ("boyfriend"); the texture, the depth, the shared past — all of that is **Sally's player's to define.** Everything below gives you *findable clues and concrete places.* Where a beat touches their relationship, it's flagged **[LOCK AT TABLE]** — offer it, let the player fill it, never narrate it for her.

**1 — Wider's list (in hand).** Names Ron as off-grid and on Serrat's queue. Sally has this already. The starting gun.

**2 — The obvious physical checks.** Ron's **Portland apartment**, his **mother's place (Lewiston)**, his old **Saco VFW** haunt. All were tossed weeks ago — *signs of a days-old professional search* (Serrat's operators, during the active hunt). **Nobody's watching them now** (Serrat pulled back), so Sally can walk in clean — a small consistency gift.
- *Portland apartment:* taped under the kitchen-sink trap — **a spot only Sally would think to check [LOCK: the hiding place comes from their relationship]** — a scrap of paper, **a single line in Ron's hand [LOCK: the player writes the sentence].** If she wants it to point somewhere, nudge it toward the **Maine coast.**

**3 — Mrs. Bennett (Lewiston, ME).** Retired schoolteacher, mid-70s, **knows nothing** about his military life. Hasn't heard from him in weeks. **Reachable in person only** — Ron told her years ago never to talk on phones. If Sally shows up:
- She **doesn't know Sally is alive** — that's the emotional gut-punch of the scene **[LOCK: let the player carry the reunion].**
- She produces a **safe-deposit-box key** Ron left her, with instructions to **"give it to Sally if anything happens to me."**

**4 — The Bangor deposit box (First Maine credit union).** Inside:
- **An encrypted USB** — Ron's **operational journal** (the foreign missions he ran; the parallel record to Wider's killing-tracker). Decryption key is **[LOCK: something only Sally would know, OR Ron tells her when she finds him].**
- **~$14,000 in cash.**
- **A folded note: three locations, one circled, two crossed out.** The circled one is **where Ron is.**

## Convergence — finding Ron

**Default: alive.** Serrat never caught him and has now given up. Sally arrives to a paranoid, well-prepared man who's been waiting for exactly one person to find him. The reunion is **[LOCK: the player's].** He has his journal, his tracking, and a face for every operator in the photos.

**Optional: dead (grief).** Only consistent if **you decide Serrat's people got him *during* the active hunt, before the cabin** — Sally arrives to a cold scene, a "heating-fault accident," his materials still hidden where the searchers didn't look. *Not* a fresh kill or a live race — that window has closed with Serrat's retreat. Use only if the player has signalled they want stakes that heavy.

**The (optional) live tension — the federal wrinkle.** If you ever want a clock back: the **FBI**, working the ODA-3223 / foreign-mission thread (via Ross), could surface Ron as a person of interest — putting the *feds*, not Serrat, on the same trail Sally's walking. Hold it in reserve; don't force it.

## What Ron is for

- **Evidence:** his operational journal + Wider's list = the strongest chain that exists; in Ross's hands it's what eventually buries Serrat (see `serrat-coldcase.md`).
- **Intel:** he can put a name and a face to the operators in any photo the PCs find.
- **If alive:** a competent extra gun (18D, his own kit) for a stretch — **not a permanent fifth PC.** His arc closes on his terms: deep cover, federal witness, or whatever the player wants.
- **If dead:** evidence, a body, and a specific cost Sally carries forward.
