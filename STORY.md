## World rule: there is no supernatural magic

Everything that reads as magic is the full expression of an ancient human-virus hybrid gene — **the helix** — integrated into *Homo sapiens* by a single stone-age community that ate a specific flower. The flowers were destroyed. The gene survived, diluted, scattered through the descendant population. Most helix-positive carriers live and die without ever expressing: lucky athletes, savants, gamblers, people who "just know."

Kallisto and Project Chimera were the first institution to quantify the mechanism and isolate the relevant sequences. The rupture on April 14 was a mass-expression event. Helix-positive bodies in range expressed fully (the four PCs, plus a small handful of others). Helix-negative bodies tried to express a gene they did not carry, and their physiology broke in the attempt — that is what every zombie, ruptured, wraith, and cocooned subject in the building actually is.

No gods answered any prayer in this world, ever. The Life Cleric's healing and Turn Undead are her helix's anti-necrotic expression. The Bard's resonance is harmonic expression. The Rogue's stealth is the seam between where the pattern is and isn't. The Warlock's "patron" is the helix's own newly-expressing voice, articulating itself in first-person-other because that is the only way his conscious mind can receive it.

## The factions

### Project Chimera (federal black programme — two compartments)

A U.S. black research programme inside the DoD/CIA black-budget perimeter. **Two compartments, separated by clearance.** Sean only knows about the first.

**The overt compartment (what Sean directs).** Real federal black-programme work in weapons biotech and human-enhancement R&D. Real DoD interface, real procurement, real personnel, real facilities. Black-budget but legible: Sean signs off on it, briefs it up to the Committee, knows his own staff. From his desk this is the entirety of Project Chimera. **Sean Erinn is its Director.** (Standing public cover: senior City Hall budget analyst — his ambient civilian identity, not a summit-week disguise.)

**The covert compartment (what Sean does not know exists).** Halder's lineage research — the helix gene, sample work on S-0 (via Antarctica), human cultivation of helix-positive subjects (via the Induction Facility basement), Halder's universal antiviral side-project. **Run by Gideon March** as broker, separately from Sean's chain. Uses **Kallisto Biomedical** as its civilian arm. Compartmented entirely outside Sean's reach — different paperwork, different personnel files, different budget lines, different physical spaces. From Sean's perspective Kallisto is an arm's-length DoD contractor whose name he had seen in peripheral reading before the summit invitation arrived.

**Personnel who serve Sean overtly and the shadow covertly:**
- **Dr. Alice Peters** — overt: Lead Applied Biogenesis Scientist, reports to Sean as his lead lab head and is also his wife. Covert: secretly seconded to Kallisto on the shadow side, reports to Gideon there. Sean does not know.
- **Dr. Henrik Loew** — overt: Programme Director, Sean's senior science manager, mid-70s. Covert: Halder's last surviving direct protégé from the late 1970s, quietly preserves Halder's antiviral side-project at low burn. Old guard. Tolerates Sean's nominal seniority because the structure protects the antiviral lineage. Will read as ally to the PCs once they reach him — slowly, carefully, on his terms.

Above the entire Chimera structure sits **Gideon March** as broker, and above Gideon sits **the Committee** (see below).

### Halder Foundation (public civilian face)

Legitimate medical-research nonprofit established in 1962 alongside the formal launch of Project Chimera. Publishes real trauma-care research, funds hospital grants, keeps a figurehead board of medical celebrities. Publicly unrelated to the federal programme it was built around. Gideon sits on its board but does not chair it. Edmund Halder (see below) holds his grandfather's family seat.

### Kallisto Biomedical (Chimera's shadow)

Private biotech in Boston. Public face: DoD-adjacent contractor and host of the annual HELIX summit. **Real function: Chimera's off-books civilian arm.** Restructured in 1977 by Gideon March out of the Halder Foundation's applied-research arm, with Halder's blessing — to create a vehicle with corporate flexibility and deniability the Foundation could not offer. Kallisto does the wet work the federal chain cannot fund or supervise; its yields feed back to Chimera without attribution.

**Public CEO:** **Dr. Vivian Crale**, late 50s. Polished, corporately competent, useful for press appearances and donor handling. Knows Kallisto is Chimera-adjacent. Does **not** know about S-0, the Antarctica site, the foreign-sales wire, or the rupture's actual mechanism. Run from below by Serrat.

**Hidden security architect:** **Col. N. Serrat** (see her own entry).

**Campus** (Boston): Building A (open-track summit and screening) and Building B (Induction Facility — see below). Connected by an enclosed glass bridge. The Induction Facility's east-end basement opens onto a Kallisto-owned private freight-rail spur to the Halder Mansion, the off-site terminus where the programme handles subjects it cannot keep on campus.

### Induction Facility (Building B)

Two cover floors above-grade (training rooms, dining, libraries, director offices, projection room) plus three basement levels of the actual programme work:

- **B1:** observation rooms, monitoring stations, low-security interrogation.
- **B2:** subject holding cells, the long-term containment block. **Iris Shane was held and experimented on here from 2024 until her death.**
- **B3:** sample-processing labs, cryogenic storage for flesh material shipped in from Antarctica, the airborne-conversion programme containers Vorshev was working out of when the rupture happened, and a sub-basement training arena for cultivated helix-positive subjects.

The east end of B3 opens onto a Kallisto-owned private freight rail platform. The rail runs to the **Halder Mansion** (see future arcs) — the programme's off-site terminus for handling subjects it cannot keep on the Boston campus, and for storing the Halder pre-1962 archive vault.

### The Antarctica Site

A permanent Halder/Kallisto research base on the Antarctic continent, built up incrementally since Halder's 1920–1945 expedition cycle (see deep history). Houses **S-0** itself — the proto-human, bound to a large circular stone by carved runes the original tribe inscribed ten thousand years ago. The runes still hold. S-0 has never moved.

The base supplies all flesh-sample material to the Induction Facility's B3 cryostore via a private secure cargo vessel — **sea transport only**, never air. Schedule is roughly quarterly. The DoD has been pressuring for direct operational control of the site for the past decade; Gideon and the Committee have so far blocked it.

(Future arc: a session on the cargo vessel is on the table.)

### Halder Genomics Initiative (HGI)

Edmund Halder's small antiviral-research outfit, affiliated with Boston University and funded through the Halder Foundation. Public face is academic. **Real mission:** finishing Everett Halder's universal antiviral programme — a compound that disables all helix-derived bioweapon agents while leaving helix-positives unharmed. Edmund has not produced a working candidate yet, but his programme is the only continuation of his grandfather's original ethical vision.

HGI is not part of Chimera, Kallisto, or the Committee structure. Edmund has not set foot inside a Kallisto facility in fifteen years. (See his NPC entry.)

### The foreign-sales wire (Serrat's side line)

Serrat has, for at least the past seven years, been moving fragments of Chimera-derived material — codon-precursor compounds, injector chemistry, operator-lock firmware, refined viral fragments — into foreign transit channels. Primary route: Frankfurt, then on to a **Russian state-affiliated biotech analogue** the Kremlin runs as its own quiet helix programme. Scattered other hops as cover.

**Buyer:** Russian state, via cut-outs. Private-market dressing on top.

**Knowledge:**
- Gideon does not know the foreign-sales pipeline exists at Serrat's scale; she runs it past him.
- The Committee does not know it exists at all (though the Committee separately knows allied-nation analogues exist and that *some* leakage has occurred — they have not connected the leak to Serrat).
- **Evelyn Ross's CIA cell has been hunting the wire for months.** Sally Burton is her asset.

### The Committee (above Gideon)

Five-seat classified American oversight body that predates Chimera by a decade. Black-budget body with serious institutional reach; keeps Chimera/Kallisto off Congressional floors.

- **The Chair** — senior figure from Treasury's Office of Intelligence and Analysis. Anonymous to staff below Gideon.
- **The DoD seat** — three-star general or civilian equivalent.
- **The intelligence seat** — CIA deputy director level.
- **The industry seat** — senior figure from the defense industrial base. Provides procurement cover.
- **The Esterhazy seat** — held by a descendant of Marian Esterhazy (see deep history). Currently a woman in her mid-60s. Veto-weighted on programme-direction questions. The only "founding lineage" seat.

**The Committee does not know:** Serrat's foreign-sales pipeline; that Halder's 1984 death was Committee-ordered (only two Committee members at the time knew, both deceased); the current state of the Halder Mansion archive vault (Gideon controls what they see).

**The Committee does know:** that allied-nation Chimera analogues exist, and that Kallisto's yields are quietly shared with some of them. They do **not** know Serrat is also feeding the same channels privately.

PCs will not encounter the Committee directly in this arc. Their presence is felt through document headers ("approved, Committee", "per Committee direction") and coded countersigns. They are a later-arc destination.

### The Listeners (cult)

40–80 members worldwide. Mostly low-expression helix-positives; a few non-carriers who married in or devoted themselves.

**Belief.** The helix gene is sacramental. The proto-humans are ancestor-saints. Halder's research and Kallisto's containment of S-0 are blasphemy. The bloodline must be protected, the captive freed, and the universal antiviral completed.

**Practices.** Collective humming sessions at 528 Hz. Abstention from medications that dampen sensory response. Pilgrimages to suspected ancient helix sites. Quiet recruitment of helix-positives into low-visibility roles.

**Leader: Anselm Yates**, early 60s. Former Kallisto junior researcher 1992–1998. Read Halder's archive (the post-1984 Halder archive opened briefly to mid-tier researchers under Gideon's reorganisation, and Yates absorbed everything he could before the access window closed). Saw the airborne-conversion programme being prepared and walked. Has led the Listeners since 2004 from the Pindos enclave (see below). His standing order to his network: **protect the bloodline first, the sacrament second, individuals last.**

**Yates ordered the Haldane killing.** See *Why Haldane was killed* below.

### The Pindos enclave

Small mountain-valley community in northern Greece (Pindos range). ~200 residents. Descendants of the stone-age flower-eater bloodline; ten thousand years of isolation and endogamy have concentrated the helix gene. Low-level expression is common in the village.

**Keeper:** **Eleni Parokhos**, mid-50s. Village teacher and midwife. Tolerates Yates and the Listener presence but is not a Listener herself. Has private reasons to distrust Kallisto.

**Iris's lineage.** Iris Shane's great-great-grandfather emigrated from this village in 1853 to Ohio. Three generations of American outbreeding diluted but did not eliminate her concentrated helix expression. Kallisto did not know the lineage when they recruited her in 2023; Gideon traced it retroactively.

**Logistical link to the foreign-sales wire.** The Boston logistics shell that fronts the Frankfurt route also handles charter cargo into Greece for Yates's network — same fleet, same shell company. The vehicle plate Haldane logged in his freight-bay file fronts both threads. This is why Yates ordered the hit (see below).

### Evelyn Ross's CIA cell

Small compartmented cell inside CIA — 8–15 people total, analysts plus a few field officers. Mandate: watch for misuse of federal bio-enhancement programmes outside Congressional oversight. Formed after an unnamed 1980s incident that nearly leaked Chimera to press. Real mandate is invisible on any badge. Ross is the senior field officer; Sally Burton is her outside asset, recruited weeks before the summit.

The rupture is the cell's first actionable break in forty years of watching. They have no knowledge of S-0, the Antarctica site, or the airborne-conversion programme — they are tracking the foreign-sales wire only.

## The Asterweiss

The original flower the stone-age community ate is destroyed except for one specimen. That one was preserved in an indestructible glass-like sphere by means the original tribe wrote about in their primitive carvings — the carving plates remain at the Antarctica site. The sphere itself is small enough to sit on a shelf. Inside, the flower has not aged.

Halder retrieved the sphere from the Antarctic site during his 1939 expedition. It has lived in the **Halder Mansion office** since 1977, hidden in plain sight as a decorative paperweight on the desk. Most visitors who notice it think it is a stylised crystal flower sculpture.

The Asterweiss is the only verified ancestral artefact of the original community outside the bound stone itself. Its botanical material — even after ten thousand years — is theoretically the seed stock for replicating the gene transfer at scale. Edmund knows the sphere exists, knows it is in the Mansion office, has not been allowed near it for fifteen years.

## S-0 — the proto-human

A stone-age human who ate the Asterweiss flower and integrated the full virus. Founded a small tribe of meta-humans by feeding the flower to others. Members who broke under the gene were terminated. The community lived in isolation under strict rules: outsiders forbidden, departures forbidden. They left primitive carved writings about the rite of consumption and the nature of those who underwent it.

The tribe collapsed in internal violence; most flowers were destroyed in the in-fighting. One specimen was preserved in the indestructible sphere (the Asterweiss). S-0 himself — the original eater, the only individual to take the unaltered virus — was bound to a circular standing stone by carved runes the surviving tribe-members inscribed before they died. The runes hold him. He has not moved in approximately ten thousand years.

**Current state.** Conscious in a way that does not match modern human cognition. Physically unable to leave the stone. Shows continuous helix expression at sustained low rate. Does not communicate in language. Kallisto has been taking flesh samples from him since the 1940s, shipping them to Boston by sea.

**On rupture day.** S-0 was undisturbed. He is still in Antarctica. His samples — held in cryogenic storage on Building B's B3 level — were what Vorshev was working with when the breach happened.

## The HELIX programme (Kallisto's 2023 cover)

Public-facing Kallisto programme launched in 2023 under the slogan *"helix initiatives — life-extension experimental options."* Pitched to academia and donor circles as Kallisto's flagship research line.

**Real function:** mass-screening cover for helix-positive identification. Every "helix initiative" applicant submits to a baseline blood panel and a battery of "wellness" cognitive/sensory tests. The panels are scored against the Halder helix profile. Positives are flagged and quietly recruited under cover stories — pattern-analytics consulting, advanced-sciences scholarships, advisory seats. Most never come out the other side.

The annual HELIX summit (where the rupture happened) is the programme's highest-profile screening event of the year — three days, twenty cohort trainees per week, multiple screening passes disguised as interviews and medical baselines.

**Cohort 47** was active on April 13–14 (placement-interview day on the 13th was when the rupture hit Building B). **Cohort 48** was the slate the four PCs were on, scheduled for the week of April 21.

## Serum codon mechanics (DM-only)

Hidden from the players. The Chimera Triage Injector and the rest of Kallisto's serum lines run on **codon-matched payloads.** Effect is determined by genotype and match status, not by what the syringe is labelled. Codons themselves are derived from S-0's flesh samples — sequences extracted, refined, and packaged in coded vials. **Each codon-locked serum has a code stamped on the vial in the form `TGC------` (six remaining bases).** PCs pick these up scattered through the facility.

| Subject genotype | Serum / event | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Helix-positive | **Matching codon** | Permanent gain of one new ability slot (class-shaped). |
| Helix-positive | **Non-matching codon** | Strip the ability in the corresponding slot. If empty, drop one level. Repeats walk the subject all the way back to baseline human. |
| Helix-negative or resistant | Any codon | Nothing. The serum metabolises and clears. |
| Helix-negative | Death | Reanimates as a broken-expression monster (zombie / hunter / wraith / cocooned). |
| Helix-positive | Death | Reanimates as a zombie. INT 6, CON 18, all attached serum-granted abilities stripped. |
| Helix-positive zombie | **Non-matching codon** | Drop one level off pre-death sheet, then stabilise back. No longer a zombie. The subject is themselves again, slower, with the loss permanent. |

**The Chimera Triage Injector** is Sally's stolen field instrument — a different device, separate lineage. It carries the programme's broad-spectrum house dose, designed to land a real match on roughly one helix-positive in four. Sally's car-stash injector has not been used at the table yet.

**The codon-locked serum the party gave Haldane** was something they picked up scattered in Building A 2/F (specific origin to be locked when reviewing the rooms). It was a non-matching codon to Haldane's sequence. That is what stabilised him at one level below his pre-death sheet.

**Codon catalogue.** Kallisto built seventeen codon strains across thirty years of work; six were ever stable enough to deploy in vivo. With S-0 still in Antarctica, the catalogue is expandable in principle — but only via fresh sample shipments, and only if the B3 sample-processing chain is still functional after the rupture.

## Deep history — 1880s to 2017

### Prehistoric (~10,000 years ago)
- **S-0 (the Progenitor)** finds and consumes the Asterweiss flower. Integrates the virus. Develops full helix expression.
- Feeds the flower to others; founds a small isolated tribe of meta-humans. Members who break under the gene are terminated; outsiders are forbidden entry; departures are forbidden.
- The tribe leaves primitive carved writings (rite, taxonomy of expression, methods of preservation, runic-binding procedures).
- The tribe collapses in internal violence. Most flowers destroyed. One preserved in the indestructible Asterweiss sphere by ritual carved into the stones.
- Surviving tribe-members bind S-0 to a circular standing stone with carved runes before they die. He cannot move.
- The site freezes over with the next glacial advance and is forgotten.

### 1880s–1900s
- Obscure physician-anthropologist (the Esterhazy family doctor) compiles careful observations of *anomalous cases* — patients with abnormal reflexes, statistically improbable intuitions, physical prodigies. Publishes in minor journals. Dies with the theory incomplete. Papers pass to the Esterhazy family.

### 1920–1945 — the Antarctic expeditions
- **Everett Halder** (then a young OSS-adjacent medical officer) leads two private Antarctic expeditions under cover of WWI/WWII strategic geological surveys. Funded quietly by the Esterhazys.
- The 1939 expedition reaches the bound site. Finds **S-0** alive on the stone, the runes still holding, and the **Asterweiss sphere** preserved beside him.
- Halder retrieves the sphere. Does not move S-0 — the runes' integrity is unknown, and he is unwilling to risk the binding.
- Documents the carved writings extensively. Establishes the first foothold of what will become the permanent Antarctica site.

### 1947–1962 — the quiet work
- Halder builds a small compartmented effort inside CIA/DoD channels, funded by the Esterhazy patronage. Continues sample collection from S-0 via expanded Antarctic facility. By 1962, he has political backing to formalise.

### 1962 — Project Chimera
- Halder formally launches Project Chimera. Founds the **Halder Foundation** as its civilian face the same year.
- Partners with **Dr. Lionel West** on wet-lab isolation of the first helix sequences from S-0 flesh samples.
- **Col. Byron Archer** runs ground operations (1962–1979).
- **Dr. Lenore Aspe** (1928–2003), virologist on the founding team, assumes the medical lead under Archer's operational cover. Will run human-experimentation programmes from 1965 until her death — Halder's reluctant compromise to keep his **antiviral side-project** funded.

### Halder's antiviral side-project (1962 onwards)
- Halder did not want to produce weapons. He sold viral-research yields to the DoD only because the DoD funding kept his real programme running.
- His real programme: develop a **universal antiviral compound** that disables all helix-derived bioweapon agents while leaving helix-positives unharmed.
- The antiviral programme has run quietly inside Chimera/Kallisto since 1962. After Halder's death it has been preserved by Loew, defunded multiple times, never fully closed. **Edmund Halder's HGI continues it from outside.**

### 1967 — West's death
- West wanted to publish. Halder and Esterhazy refused. West threatened public disclosure. Died in a staged "lab explosion." Notebooks absorbed into Halder's archive.

### 1971 — Gideon arrives
- Halder recruits **Gideon March** out of a DoD procurement office. Grooms him through the decade as personal protégé.

### 1977 — Kallisto founded
- Gideon restructures the Halder Foundation's applied-research arm into **Kallisto Biomedical** with Halder's blessing. The Asterweiss sphere is moved from a Foundation safe to the **Halder Mansion office** at the same time. **Henrik Loew** joins as a junior researcher under Halder.

### 1984 — Halder's death
- Officially a stroke. In reality: Halder's grandson — **Edmund Halder, then age 11** — had been exposed to a prototype helix treatment at a Halder Foundation paediatric trial and was showing early helix expression. Halder's grief over what the programme had done to his own bloodline cracked his patience. In his last months he began drafting a partial exposure document.
- The Committee learned. Two members ordered him poisoned. Both members are now deceased; nobody currently sitting on the Committee knows the truth.
- Gideon inherited the succession believing the stroke story.

### 1984 onwards — Gideon's era
- Gideon has run the shadow side for forty-plus years.
- Loew preserves Halder's antiviral programme inside Chimera at low burn.
- Edmund Halder grows up as the program's open secret — a helix-positive child of the founder's own line. Privately monitored. Publicly told his early childhood "illness" was a childhood leukaemia he beat. Pulled away from the mansion in his teens by his mother (Halder's daughter-in-law, who knew enough to take the boy out of the family operations orbit).

### 1992–1998 — Yates inside Kallisto
- **Anselm Yates** joins Kallisto as a junior researcher 1992. Reads what he can of Halder's archive during a window of internal access. Witnesses early airborne-conversion experimental work in 1997. Walks in 1998. Spends the next six years building the Listener network.

### 2004 — Listeners formalised
- Yates settles at the Pindos enclave. Begins formal Listener recruitment.

### 2008 — Pindos welcomes Yates
- Eleni Parokhos extends the village's protection to him. He has operated from the enclave continuously since.

### 2017 — Haldane joins Kallisto
- **Victor Haldane** hired as Building A security lead. Reports to Mara Kessler operationally; reports to Serrat covertly through her 18D-commander hat.

## Recent history — 2018 to April 13, 2026

### 2018–2024 — Serrat's foreign-sales wire
- Serrat builds the Frankfurt route through a Boston logistics shell. Material flows out: codon precursors, injector chemistry, operator-lock firmware, refined viral fragments. The buyer is a Russian state-affiliated biotech analogue.
- **The same logistics shell handles charter cargo into the Pindos enclave for Yates's network.** Yates knows about the shell because he set up the Pindos channel through it before Serrat existed; Serrat does not know Yates is co-using the shell, and Yates does not know Serrat is using it for Frankfurt. Both the foreign-sales records and the Pindos supply records sit in the same company books. (This is what makes Haldane's freight-bay file lethal in 2026.)

### 2023 — three convergent events
- **Iris Shane recruited.** Flagged by Kallisto's pattern-analytics screen for anomalous pattern-recognition scores. Recruited under a "pattern-analytics consulting" cover via a Chimera-adjacent shell. Genotyped on intake — comes back as the **first verified intact helix-positive natural bloodline** Kallisto has ever recovered. Her ancestral link to the Pindos enclave is traced retroactively by Gideon. Kept under cover roles for her first year while her expression is cultivated.
- **Peters runs Sean's blood panel.** Routine spousal panel or targeted curiosity. Sean comes back helix-positive with an unusual expression profile. Peters understands what this means immediately — Kallisto's screening apparatus will identify the same flag through any non-Peters channel within months, and Serrat's security playbook is to remove rather than process. Peters goes to Gideon and asks for protection.
- **Kallisto's screening flags Sean independently** during a separate routine pull (he is, after all, a Chimera Director — his medical baseline gets re-pulled as an oversight matter). Two flags arrive on Gideon's desk in the same quarter: Peters's confidential request, and the screening hit. Gideon now has a problem he can shape rather than a problem he must report up.

### 2023 — the HELIX programme launches
- Kallisto launches the public **HELIX initiatives** programme as a screening cover. Annual summits begin. First cohort placed.

### 2023 — Gideon brokers Sean's path
- Gideon goes to Serrat before Serrat finds the screening flag through Kallisto's normal escalation. Proposes **Halder Mansion processing** as the controlled alternative to elimination — a quiet transfer to the off-site terminus where the programme handles subjects it cannot keep on the Boston campus. Serrat agrees reluctantly. Papers stay unsigned for now — Gideon wants more time before triggering the move.
- Gideon does not tell Peters the plan. Peters believes Sean is being informally watched. Peters keeps working — partly because of Sean, partly because Sean is a capable Director and his career advances are tied to her productivity. Win-win, in her own framing. (The compromise will eat her alive when it matures, but for now she works.)

### 2024 — Iris into the basement
- Iris's expression has reached a level Kallisto can productively cultivate. She is moved to **B2 of the Induction Facility basement** under permanent containment. Officially: extended medical observation. In practice: experimental subject, run on aggressive protocols.
- Her flashes of knowledge (a side-effect of cultivated expression — fragmentary intuitions, names, images, sentences she has no source for) are transcribed by staff. The transcripts sit in Kallisto's archive. None of them are predictions about S-0 — Iris has never been near S-0, knows S-0 only as "the source" in laboratory paperwork. Her flashes about the four PCs, about the "black horned hexagon," about the door — those are real, but they come from her expression itself, not from a relationship with S-0.

### 2024–2025 — Vorshev's defection brews
- **Dr. Adrian Vorshev** (b. 1968), senior virologist running the Induction Facility basement labs and lead on the **airborne-conversion research programme**, begins skimming S-0 flesh samples for personal stash.
- Vorshev is helix-positive himself — discovered late, never disclosed. He has spent two decades quietly forming the conviction that the gene is humanity's destined next stage and the programme has been crawling when it should be running.
- He opens a private channel to Kallisto's Russian counterpart (the same buyer as Serrat's wire, but Vorshev does not know Serrat is also selling — they run separate covers). Plans to defect, fake his death, and emerge with private resources to push his own accelerationist work.

### Early February 2026 — Haldane's freight anomaly
- Haldane catches Building A's secondary loading bay running unauthorised after-hours freight at 02:14. Naomi Varga's badge plus a no-record badge. Cargo into an unmarked private medical-transport vehicle, no manifest. He logs plate, badge IDs, timestamps; files a protocol-violation report. Kessler closes it *addressed at director level — close*. (Truth: vessel-pipeline transfers — past-cohort holdovers being moved by road to the Halder Mansion for off-site processing.)

### February–April 2026 — Haldane's private file
- Two months of quiet archive pulls. Seven matching freight events in the prior twelve months. Three vehicle plates, one repeat. **One of the three plates traces, two queries downstream, to the Boston logistics shell that fronts both Serrat's Frankfurt route and Yates's Pindos supply.** Haldane never pulled the registration; he just wrote the number down.

### April 11, 2026 — Haldane pushes Kessler
- Haldane walks into Kessler's office and asks plainly what the original closure had meant. Kessler stalls, ushers him out, calls **Yates's broker** — not Serrat. (Detail: Yates has a long-standing intelligence asset inside Kessler's office, a quiet clerical assistant who has been feeding him movement data on Kessler's calls and visitors for years; the call out was routine, and Yates received a flag the same day.)
- Yates reads the freight pattern as imminent compromise of the Pindos supply line. If Haldane's file ever reaches compliance, the audit follows the plate, finds the shell, finds Pindos. Kallisto identifies the enclave and consumes the ~200 helix-positive descendants as a vessel reserve over the next decade. Yates trades Haldane's life for theirs. Hires **Elias Varo** through a contractor broker layer — Varo never knows who paid.

### April 13, 2026 — Cohort 47, day three
- Cohort 47 placement-interview day at the Induction Facility. Twenty trainees, three days in residence.
- Vorshev is in the basement labs running the airborne-conversion programme. Plans to extract a fresh personal stash of refined airborne-form viral material that night and disappear into his exit network the next morning.

### April 14, 2026 — the morning before the rupture
- Kallisto's annual HELIX summit, day one of the public schedule, runs in Building A.
- The four PCs converge on Building A under their separate covers.
- Vorshev attempts the extraction at the airborne-conversion containers on Building B's B3 level roughly mid-morning. **He is caught mid-skim by a B3 technician.** In the panic he leaves a container ajar and the secondary safety vent system, already tampered for his exit, malfunctions. The breach goes undetected for several minutes. The airborne agent leaks into the basement ventilation reserve.
- Vorshev flees the campus before security closes. **Reaches the Halder Mansion that afternoon** and goes to ground there. (Why the mansion? It is a Kallisto-secure off-grid site, his clearances still nominally work, he had cached resources there. He intends to ride out the cover storm and exit the country within a week.)

## Rupture day — April 14, 2026

### Iris's death (~14:00)
- Iris had been refusing IVs and medications for several days. By the morning of the 14th she is too weak to sit up.
- Her body's cumulative experimental burn has reached terminal load. Around **14:00** her organs give out. She dies in her cell in B2.
- Her death is **not** the rupture trigger. (It is not unrelated either — her flashes had warned of a "venting" event in the days before, and Kallisto staff were cycling readings at higher frequency that morning because of those warnings. None of them connected the warnings to Vorshev.)
- Body still in B2.

### The rupture (14:23)
- The B3 containment system has been venting agent into the basement ventilation reserve for roughly forty minutes by the time the secondary safety logic finally kicks in.
- The safety logic, instead of containing, **routes the reserve outward through the campus HVAC** — a fail-open mode meant for chemical contamination from outside, repurposed catastrophically by the tampered settings Vorshev left.
- At **14:23** the airborne viral agent vents through Building A and Building B simultaneously.

## Mandatory pre-write checklist

Apply before writing any room, clue, or NPC scene.

### 1. Timeline stamp
- **Rupture:** April 14, 14:23.
- **Current PC time:** April 15, mid-morning. **Approximately 18–20 hours after the rupture.**
- Anything an NPC says about "today / this morning / earlier / after the event" is computed against April 15 — not April 14. An interview that happened to Sean was *yesterday*, not this morning. A recording made April 14 says "today" from within its own frame but is, to the PCs, *yesterday's recording*.

### 2. NPC state check
For every NPC the PCs meet, answer:
- Where has this NPC physically been for the last 18+ hours?
- Have they eaten, slept, drunk water? (Almost certainly no or barely.)
- What have they heard, seen, or tried to do since 14:23?
- What do they think Kallisto is, as an institution, by now?

### 3. Realism check
An 18-hour post-disaster NPC is not running office-hours etiquette. They are dehydrated, hungry, sleep-deprived, jumpy, cycling between composure and panic, suspicious of anyone walking in, desperate for information rather than polite conversation, aware their world ended yesterday.

If I am writing an NPC asking polite *what brings you here* questions, I am writing a person who hasn't lived the last 18 hours. Stop and rewrite.

### 4. Concrete-language rule
Staff notes must say what the subject would actually say or do, not a category label for it. Real people write specifically because the next person who reads the note has to act on it.

Forbidden: invented hyphenated noun-phrases that compress observations into single terms (*"foreign-transfer language,"* *"procurement vocabulary,"* *"ask pattern,"* *"authority-failure prompts"*). If I have written one, stop and rewrite with what a staffer would actually put on the page.

### 5. Fact-check gate (Rule 0)
Before writing any clue that describes what a PC did, said, or responded to:
- If the event was **not RP'd at the table**, the clue cannot reference it as an observation. Paperwork can say *"pending review,"* *"advance per routing,"* *"not yet screened,"* or state the scheduled procedural fact without claiming a behavioural observation.
- If tempted to write *"S.E. committed in 00:08 to order-conflicts"* or *"his hand went to the orange ring"* — stop. Those are fabrications of player performance. Rewrite as procedure or drop.

### 6. No invented corporate jargon
"Off-books patient transport for board family" is invented. So is "billing scandal." Real security professionals log *unauthorised after-hours freight movement at the secondary loading bay*. They do not theorise about contents.

### 7. Voice audit
Read the line once. *Could a real person in this situation, in this emotional state, actually say or write this?* If only a GM narrator could say it, rewrite.
