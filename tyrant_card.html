A massive humanoid stripped to its mutant core. Pale grey-purple flesh stretched over an inhuman frame; one arm grown into a recurved bone-blade as long as a man is tall. The chest cavity is open. The heart beats wet behind a cage of split ribs. The face above the missing jaw is recognisable. The eyes are not.

Tyrant

Large aberration (transformation), unaligned

Armor Class 17 (mutated hide)

Hit Points 248 (16d10 + 160)

Challenge 12 (8,400 XP)   Proficiency Bonus +4

Magic Resistance. The Tyrant has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.
Regeneration. The Tyrant regains 15 hit points at the start of its turn if it has at least 1 hit point and hasn't taken radiant damage since the end of its last turn.
Exposed Heart. The Tyrant's vital organs are visible in its open chest cavity. A creature can target the heart specifically with a ranged or melee weapon attack; doing so imposes disadvantage on the attack roll, but on a hit the attack scores a critical hit on a roll of 18, 19, or 20 (in addition to dealing the normal damage of a critical hit). When the Tyrant takes 25 damage or more from a single critical hit targeting the heart, its Regeneration trait doesn't function at the start of its next turn.
Rampage. At the end of its turn, if no creature is within 10 feet of it, the Tyrant uses its reaction to move up to its speed toward the nearest creature it can sense and makes one Massive Claw attack against that creature. The Tyrant does not distinguish friend from foe.
Wretched Pull. While any creature carrying the helix gene is within 60 feet of it, the Tyrant has disadvantage on attack rolls against any creature that is not carrying the helix gene.
Reckless. At the start of its turn, the Tyrant can gain advantage on all melee weapon attack rolls it makes during that turn, but attack rolls against it have advantage until the start of its next turn.

Actions

Multiattack. The Tyrant makes three attacks: two with its Massive Claw and one with its Devouring Bite.
Massive Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (3d10 + 7) slashing damage. On a critical hit, the target must succeed on a DC 19 Strength saving throw or be thrown 15 feet from the Tyrant and knocked prone.
Devouring Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 20 (3d8 + 7) piercing damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 19 Strength saving throw or be grappled (escape DC 19). Until this grapple ends, the target is restrained, and the Tyrant can't bite another target. While a creature is grappled by the Tyrant's bite, the Tyrant has advantage on attack rolls against it.
Lunge (Recharge 5–6). The Tyrant moves up to 30 feet in a straight line. Each creature in its path must make a DC 19 Dexterity saving throw, taking 33 (6d10) bludgeoning damage and being knocked prone on a failed save, or half as much damage and not prone on a successful one. The Tyrant must end this movement in an unoccupied space along the line.
Frightful Roar (Recharge 6). The Tyrant emits a sub-harmonic roar. Each creature of the Tyrant's choice within 60 feet of it that can hear it must succeed on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of the Tyrant for 1 minute. A frightened creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. Creatures that share the helix gene with the Tyrant's host are immune to this effect.

Legendary Actions

The Tyrant can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Tyrant regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

Stride. The Tyrant moves up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Claw Attack. The Tyrant makes one Massive Claw attack.
Wretched Cry (Costs 2 Actions). The Tyrant emits a brief sub-harmonic pulse. Each creature within 30 feet of it that can hear it must succeed on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw or take 11 (2d10) thunder damage and have its speed halved until the end of its next turn.
