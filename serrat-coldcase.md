# Serrat — Cold Case (optional, player-driven)

After the cabin, **Serrat is off the board as an active threat.** He dropped the hunt and went to ground. This file is the skeleton if the players *choose* to chase him or the evidence. ~80% they won't — don't push it.

## Where he stands now

- His forward New England element died at the cabin; Hatch fled; **his helicopter is now a federal crime scene** (a black aircraft registered to his West Mountain shell, down in the no-fly zone next to his dead men). The cover-up he murdered his own operators to protect is blown.
- He's severing his trail remotely: torch the West Mountain paper, **strip and burn the Spofford safehouse before the FBI raids it.**
- Klaus would happily hang the whole thing on him as "the rogue colonel who ran an unauthorized network." Serrat's shield is his **private file on Kallisto's covert compartment** — *touch me and it goes public.* A quiet war between them, both under federal heat. Self-preservation now eats all his other motives; he runs for his Geneva / Mironov exit.
- **He is not chasing the PCs.** He hasn't the assets, it'd be suicidal, and it's pointless now the feds are the ones closing.

## The dilemma (the good hook)

The PCs hold **Wider's list** — the evidence that nails Serrat. But the only people who can *use* it are the feds / **Evelyn Ross** — the same net hunting the PCs. **To bury Serrat, they'd have to walk evidence into the net's hands.** That's a real choice, and it's theirs. No push.

## How it pays off by default (without the PCs lifting a finger)

Through **Evelyn Ross + the federal investigation.** Ross's CIA cell already owns the foreign-sales angle that reaches Serrat (canon). If the evidence trickles to her — via Sally eventually, or the FBI processing the crash — Serrat gets taken down off-screen.

## If they DO chase him (how to reignite)

- **The trail:** Wider's list → the crash-scene shell (West Mountain Holdings) → the Spofford safehouse (cold, likely torched — if they move fast, scraps remain) → a pencilled dot near the Maine/Quebec border (where Serrat actually is, briefly, before he runs).
- **Cornered, he's a desperate man** covering tracks and angling for a deal — not the confident spymaster. He might even try to turn the PCs against Klaus.
- The full pre-redesign clue/loot/safehouse detail (the kill ledger prop, the West Mountain → GSPG chain, the Chimera-medkit links, the operator roster) lives in **git history of the old root `next-session.md`** (the pre-folder version) if you want to pull it back.

## Parked sub-threads

- **Ron Bennett (Sally's personal arc):** on Wider's list as off-grid and hunted. With Serrat's cleanup abandoned, Ron's *immediate* danger recedes — but Sally can still go looking. Her call, low urgency now.
- **L / Mironov / the Frankfurt route:** Serrat's exit infrastructure. Matters only if the PCs (or the feds) chase the foreign-sales pipeline.
- **The Committee's hand in the federal net:** this is the live, dangerous version of the Serrat-era antagonism — it has moved *up* a level (see `next-session/base.md`). The Committee, not Serrat, is now the malice steering guns at the PCs.
