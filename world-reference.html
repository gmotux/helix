Identity

Kallisto Biomedical

Public Face
A premium biomedical firm focused on adaptive therapies, emergency response bioengineering, resilience systems, neural recovery, and clinical innovation.
Real Nature
A convergence platform joining disaster medicine, municipal continuity planning, private capital, black-budget biosystems work, and post-human theory under one institution.
Core Premise
Conventional institutions and conventional human physiology are not sufficient for the urban crises Kallisto believes are already emerging.
Method
Selective consent, compartmentalization, aggressive field testing, and emergency logic that permits irreversible intervention.
Practical Read

Institutional Tone

  • Clinical rather than theatrical.
  • Sincere about survival, ruthless about tradeoffs.
  • Capable of care and exploitation at the same time.
  • Internally divided, never fully unified.
Motivations

Institutional Goal

Build continuity tools for systemic collapse: medical, civic, and biological.

Profit matters, but survival legitimacy matters more.

Fear

That the coming crises will outpace law, medicine, and infrastructure before anyone is willing to act.

Blind Spot

Belief that being early, secretive, and smarter than everyone else justifies crossing thresholds no public institution could survive politically.

Internal Structure

Preservationists

Keep institutions functional.

Use biotech as shield, stabilizer, and continuity instrument.

Willing to authorize extreme measures if collapse appears imminent.

Adaptationists

Change humans fast enough to survive new conditions.

View baseline humanity as too fragile for the environment ahead.

Sound brutally practical rather than openly cruel.

Transcendents

Engineer a successor state of human life.

View the crisis as proof that inherited humanity is an obsolete base model.

Most likely to embrace irreversible transformation as desirable.

Session 0

Ignition Model

Public Layer
HELIX week is a real Boston summit for donors, hospital partners, regulators, investors, and selected clinicians. The public event exists mainly as cover, traffic, and legitimacy.
PC Rule
The PCs are not a shared keynote audience by default. Each one intersects Kallisto through a private lane: consult, screening, site visit, briefing, audit, recruitment conversation, covert tasking, or personal lead.
Ignition Time
The ignition occurs at Friday, 14:23 Boston time. It happens whether the PCs are in Helix Tower, Orpheus Annex, a car, a side meeting, or already walking away.
Solo Format
Each Session 0 is a solo prelude ending five to fifteen minutes before 14:23. Session 1 starts with every PC at that exact timestamp, each in a different Boston location tied to their own lane.
Common Shape
Approach, private contact, one suspicious detail, one human interaction that matters, one decision point, then ignition. The prelude gives motive and context, not party assembly.
Kallisto Need
Kallisto does not need all PCs in one room. It only needs them in contact with its systems, staff, facilities, or trigger environment by 14:23.

Deviation Handling

If Players Refuse The Planned Path

Walk Out Early
Let them. The ignition still lands at 14:23 and catches them in transit, on the street, or near the waterfront.
Refuse Exams
Reroute them into a meeting, hospitality hold, private consult, or observation room. They do not need to complete the same procedure as everyone else.
Never Reach Helix Tower
Move their lane to Orpheus Annex, a hotel meeting room, a port transfer, a rideshare handoff, or a surveillance post near Kallisto traffic.
Go Loud
Security pressure rises, but the main consequence is location change, not campaign derailment. The 14:23 ignition remains fixed.
