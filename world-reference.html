Story So Far

Where The Party Is Right Now

The Rupture

April 14, 14:23. Kallisto Biomedical's annual summit at the JOINN Innovation Park campus. SC-17 (Iris Shane) — the only mind able to stabilise the proto-human source S-0 — died of organ failure around 14:00. Kallisto did not yet know, and ran the scheduled extraction on a dying organism. The chamber vented at 14:23.

Helix-positive bodies (the four PCs) expressed the gene fully and survived as themselves. Helix-negative bodies tried to express a sequence they did not carry and broke — zombies, hunters, ruptured, cocooned. A field of black mist rolled out across the campus. The "magic" the PCs now experience is the full expression of an ancient human-virus hybrid gene; it is biological, not occult.

Through Building A

The four PCs woke into Building A's testing floor. They cleared a stair-entry pack, recovered the bridge key and ratchet from Haldane's office in 299, and left Greta Solheim and Marta Ibarra alive on 1/F. Cohort 48 — the next week's intake — was the slate the PCs were on. They never sat the interview.

On the way out they explored the last 2/F rooms, fought a hard fight on the bridge against rupture-broken staff, and killed an outside contractor working the corridor — Elias Varo, sent under an early-termination clause. He died without giving up his sender. The bridge is behind them now.

Haldane's Turn

Victor Haldane, Building A's security lead, was already a body before the party ever met him — killed in his own office in 299 the morning of the rupture on a directive the party found pinned to him. When the field vented at 14:23 his helix-positive body reanimated and walked into the bridge corridor. The party encountered him there as a zombie among the rupture-broken, fought through, and the Doctor administered a Chimera Triage Injector dose rather than finish him. The wrong codon dropped him one level off his old sheet and pulled him back across the line. Haldane is now awakened: lucid enough to follow, slow to speak, watchful in a way he was not in life. See Why Haldane Was Killed for the deeper read; see Serum Codon Mechanics for what the serum actually did.

Building B Now

The party stands at the west entry of Building B 2/F — the Kallisto Induction Facility. Upstairs is onboarding theatre for new hires; the basement levels are where the program does its real work on people the Halder Foundation cannot acknowledge. Cohort 47 was here on placement-interview day when the rupture hit. Director "Dr. Moira Vance" was off-site at Building A and is alive. Assistant Director Hannah Reeve is dead in Narrow Corridor A.

The exit out of the campus is the Kallisto-owned freight rail at the far east of the building. The next stop down the line is the Halder House — and that is the next session.

World Spine

Story Elements That Are Load-Bearing

The Helix Gene

Stone-age origin: one community ate a flower carrying a human-compatible virus. Their descendants now carry trace helix expression — athletic outliers, savants, "lucky" people. Kallisto and Chimera spent decades isolating the sequences. The rupture was a mass-expression event. Helix-positive bodies expressed fully (the four PCs). Helix-negative bodies tried to express a gene they did not carry and broke.

There is no occult system in this world. Cleric healing and Turn Undead are anti-necrotic helix expression. Bard is harmonic expression. Rogue is the seam — attention sliding through the gaps. Warlock is the helix's own internal voice articulating itself to its host as an other. None of this is divine. None of it is bargained.

S-0, The Sleeper

Proto-human. The flower-eater. Alive-ish, contained at Orpheus Annex, conscious, unable to speak, suffering. Kallisto has been drawing from it for years. Iris Shane was its first and only interpreter in 10,000 years; her ongoing contact stabilised the organism. Kallisto thought she was a data-quality asset.

On rupture day S-0 died. The chamber vented. The party does not yet know S-0 exists by name. Iris's clue work is mercy for the source — she wants S-0 killed in kindness and put to rest. The party are her instrument, addressed obliquely, never by callsign-roster.

The Mist Field

The vented field rolled outward from Orpheus Annex through the campus. Inside the field, helix-negative dead rise once and walk; rooms drift; the air carries a residue the PCs call black dust. Don't stand over the bodies when they vent.

Each PC has a once-per-encounter mist-survival action — Bernard's Harmonic Hum, Sally's Hold the Door, Sean's Pact Utterance, the Killer's Through the Seam. Action cost, three-round window, self-plus-ally scope. See the Mist-Survival Actions panel below.

Why Haldane Was Killed

Position. Kessler's security lead, Building A, nine years. No clearance for Project Chimera, Orpheus Annex, or Sean Erinn's role.

The anomaly. Early February, covering a night shift, 02:14. Building A's secondary loading bay (off the public floorplan) ran an unauthorised after-hours freight movement. Two badges scanned: Naomi Varga and a man whose badge returned no record. Cargo into an unmarked private medical-transport vehicle, out the back gate, no manifest. Haldane logged the plate, badge IDs, and timestamps, and filed a protocol-violation report. Kessler closed it addressed at director level. No follow-up.

The pattern. Two months of quiet archive pulls. Seven matching events in the prior twelve months. Same time window. No-record badge on five. Varga on all seven. Three vehicle plates, one repeat. April 11: walked into Kessler's office, asked plainly what the closure had meant. Kessler stalled, ushered him out, called Serrat the same afternoon.

What the transfers actually are (DM-side). Vessel-pipeline moves to Orpheus Annex. The program's research at OA — codon extraction from live tissue, interpreter conditioning, long-form expression studies — burns through helix-positive subjects in one to three years and needs continuous replacements. Recruitment runs through summit weeks: candidates who screen helix-positive are kept on the books as under extended medical observation in restricted holding rooms until OA has a containment slot, then sedated, bagged, and driven out at 02:00–03:00 from the secondary bay. Families get standard covers (overseas posting, medical complications, unexpected resignation). Iris Shane went through this pipeline three years ago. Cadence: roughly one transfer every six to eight weeks.

Why the kill order. One of the three plates in Haldane's file registers — two queries downstream — to a Boston logistics shell fronting a German pharmaceutical broker. That broker is the front end of Serrat's unauthorised foreign-sales pipeline (Frankfurt route, seven years, Committee unaware). The Committee handles its own leaks terminally. Compliance review of Haldane's file would surface the plate within a week. The vessel pipeline alone would not have justified a hit; the foreign-sales line did.

The hit. Outside hand: Elias Varo, summit-week auditor credentials. 09:15 April 14: knock on 299, suppressed sidearm from the side, directive placed on the desk as case closure. Varo was to return for the page once the rupture cover settled. The rupture closed his exit corridor; he pushed east, ended up among the broken on the bridge, party killed him without leads.

Helix status. Dormant helix-positive, never expressed in life. Rupture brought the body back walking. The Doctor's non-matching codon dose dropped him one level below his pre-death sheet (see Serum Codon Mechanics).

Awakened recall. Plates, timestamps, the bay route, his April 11 visit to Kessler. Cannot identify Varo (stranger at the door, shot from the side, no clean look at a face). Directive names Serrat; Varo is unconnected.

File. Hollow under the desk drawer in 299. Three plates written on the inside of the manila folder; the Frankfurt plate is one of them.

DM Only

Serum Codon Mechanics

Hidden from the players. The Chimera Triage Injector and the rest of the program's serum lines run on codon-matched payloads. Effect is determined by genotype and match status, not by what the syringe is labelled. The party will infer pieces of this through what they see happen to bodies — never reveal the table directly.

Helix-Positive + Matching Codon

Permanent gain. The injection unlocks a new ability slot — typically a class-shaped one (extra invocation, channel-divinity option, bardic flourish, rogue feature). The body integrates the codon as if it had always been there. No save, no cost, no rejection.

Helix-Positive + Non-Matching Codon

The body fights the wrong sequence and burns its own expression to do it. Strip the ability in the corresponding slot. If the slot is already empty, drop one level instead. Repeat administrations cost ability after ability, level after level, until the subject is a baseline human again. This is how Kallisto unwinds a vessel it no longer wants.

Helix-Negative or Resistant

Nothing happens. The serum metabolises and clears within a few hours. No record, no payload, no expression — the codon has nothing to bind to. From the outside the injection looks identical to a successful one, which is sometimes the point.

Helix-Negative Death

Reanimates as a monster — the broken-expression family the rupture made wholesale (zombies of the wraith / hunter / ruptured / cocooned strain). Same biological mechanic as the 14:23 rupture, scaled to a single body. Death does not care whether the cause was injury, illness, or violence.

Helix-Positive Death

Reanimates as a zombie within minutes. INT drops to 6, CON rises to 18, all attached serum-granted abilities are stripped (the tether to the original sequence is gone). The body is hostile, slow, durable, and remembers nothing. This is the mechanic that brought Haldane back on the bridge.

Helix-Positive Zombie + Non-Matching Codon

The wrong sequence forces a partial re-anchor. Drop one level off the pre-death sheet, then stabilise back to the rest of those stats. The subject is no longer a zombie. They are themselves again — slower, weaker, with the loss permanent. This is what the party did to Haldane on the bridge. Neither side knew that was what was happening.

Where Codons Come From

Every codon Kallisto fields is derived from S-0's tissue. The proto-human is the only known unbroken expression of the gene, and the program's biochemistry is, at root, controlled extraction from a single subject. Iris Shane's interpretive work was the bridge: she held S-0 stable enough that the labs could run extractions without crashing the organism. With Iris dead and S-0 dead, no new codons can be made. The seventeen-strain catalogue Kallisto built over thirty years is now a closed set; six of those strains were ever stable enough to deploy in vivo. The rest live as frozen archives, waiting for an interpreter that no longer exists.

The Triage Injector Specifically

The dose Sally carries — and the one she gave Haldane — is the program's house broad-spectrum: a blended payload built to hit roughly one helix-positive in four with a real match, deliberately tuned to drop the other three by one level rather than catastrophic strip-out. It is the field analogue of a tourniquet, not a scalpel. It is also why Haldane stabilised exactly one level below his pre-death sheet rather than further: the broad-spectrum is too crude to walk a body all the way back to baseline human in a single dose, and Kallisto designed it that way on purpose. Anyone the party hits with this injector is going to end up close to themselves, slightly diminished, and quiet about it.

5e Field Items

Chimera Triage Injector

Consumable prototype

Single-use spring injector in a matte black sleeve stamped with the Chimera mark.

Use: Bonus action on yourself, or action on a willing creature within 5 feet.

Effect: The target gains 6 temporary hit points, immediately ends the frightened condition if it came from shock or pain, and has advantage on Constitution saving throws for 1 minute. If the target is at 0 hit points, it instead rises to 1 hit point and also gains the temporary hit points.

Crash: When the minute ends, the target makes a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, it gains one level of exhaustion.

Table Use: Military emergency chemistry, not a healing potion. It keeps someone moving through one bad fight and then makes them pay for it.

Chimera Aster-9

Martial ranged weapon, firearm, prototype

Polymer-frame 9mm sidearm with a dull ceramic slide, flush optic notch, and a grip sensor that glows red when the wrong hand closes on it.

Damage: 2d6 piercing.

Range: 40/120.

Magazine: 12 shots. Reloading with a fresh magazine takes an action.

Operator Lock: A keyed user fires it normally. An unkeyed user gets a dead trigger, a red grip-light, and an audible lock chirp that can be heard out to the next room.

Seizure Rule: A creature can bypass the lock only by spending 10 minutes with tinker's tools or thieves' tools and succeeding on a DC 15 Dexterity check. On a failure, the pistol hard-locks until the end of a short rest.

Mist-Survival Actions

Each PC's newly-expressed helix trait lets them push back the mist for a short window. One class resource per use, same action economy, parallel scope. The hazard DC and the mist's per-round timing stay with the GM.

Bernard — Harmonic Hum

Bard / Singer

Bernard keys a sustained hum to 528 Hz — the pitch the mist refuses. A single listener finds a breath of clean air under the tone.

Cost: 1 Bardic Inspiration die (the die still functions as a normal Bardic Inspiration if the recipient uses it on a later roll).

Action: Action. Verbal.

Effect: One ally of Bernard's choice within 30 ft gains immunity to mist exposure on their next mist saving throw.

Sally — Hold the Door

Life Cleric / Doctor

Her healing magic pushes the field back. Whenever Sally casts a cleric spell of 1st level or higher, or spends a use of Channel Divinity, the mist within thirty feet of her retreats for the duration (up to one minute).

Cost: None beyond the spell or Channel Divinity itself.

Action: Property of her casting, not a separate action.

Effect: Allies within 30 ft of Sally are treated as mist-immune for the duration of the spell or channel. If she expends a use of Channel Divinity specifically to hold the door (no other spell effect), one ally of her choice within 30 ft also regains 1d4 hit points.

Sean — Pact Utterance

Warlock / Director

His casting carries pact magic the field cannot hold. Whenever Sean casts a Warlock spell or cantrip with a verbal component, the mist within fifteen feet of him is pushed out.

Cost: None beyond the spell or cantrip itself.

Action: Property of his casting, not a separate action.

Effect: Mist within 15 ft of Sean is cleared for the spell duration (minimum 1 round). Allies in that radius are treated as mist-immune for the duration. Cantrips cleanse for 1 round; spells of 1st level or higher hold for the spell duration or 1 minute, whichever is longer.

The Killer — Through the Seam

Rogue / Killer

She reads the space where the pattern isn't and walks there. Those close enough to follow her step walk through the same seam.

Cost: 2 uses per short rest.

Action: Action.

Effect: The Killer and any allies within 10 ft of her at the moment of use may together move up to 60 ft through mist-occupied space without rolling any mist saving throws during that movement.

Mist Field

Usable Rules

Building A State

Inside: no full ambient mist field yet.

Inside effects: short death-plumes, localized anomaly rooms, and contaminated air only where a room has gone bad.

Outside: the campus already holds a heavier residue field because too many altered bodies, dead zones, and active anomalies overlap there.

Death-Plume Rule

Whenever a changed human dies, it exhales a short black cloud in a 5-foot radius.

The cloud usually fades by the end of the next round unless nearby anomalies or destructive awakened output keep feeding it.

Area Cleansing

An area is mostly cleansed when hostile mutants are dead, active anomalies are shut down, and any remaining changed people are either calm, moved, or stabilized.

Killing a sympathetic changed person also works, but it is the brutal option.

Power Direction

Destructive awakened output: local residue lasts longer, thickens faster, and makes the room harsher.

Protective, healing, light, or calming output: local residue thins faster and anomalies become easier to shut down.

Mist Exposure

Make a Constitution save when a PC is within 5 feet of a changed human as it dies, enters heavy exterior residue, starts a turn near a major anomaly, or uses destructive awakened output inside active mist.

DCs: 10 death-plume, 12 ambient mist, 14 anomaly surge or heavy district. Add +2 after destructive awakened output.

Mist Marks

Fail: gain 1 Mist Mark. Fail by 5 or more: also take 1d4 poison damage.

1: cough, sting, watering eyes.

2: disadvantage on next Perception or Concentration check.

3: speed reduced by 10 feet.

4: poisoned until cleared.

Clearing Mist Marks

Remove 1 mark with clean shelter, water, and 10 minutes of rest; with medical treatment; or with cleansing/protective magic.

Clear all marks with a full rest in a clean zone or after a successful coherence-style cleansing rite.

Table Use

Run Building A as the first place the party learns the rule.

No pre-event document in Building A should already explain the rule. The party learns it from fresh notes, witness accounts, death-plumes, and post-14:23 observations.
